# Stakeholder Correspondence: Templates and Guidance for Every Audience

Every piece of campaign communication either builds or erodes a relationship. This module provides templates, tone guidance, and channel-specific formats for writing to every stakeholder type a campaign encounters.

---

## General Principles

1. **Lead with the relationship, not the ask.** People give time, money, and support to candidates they feel connected to, not candidates who only call when they need something.
2. **Be specific.** "Thank you for your generous contribution" is weaker than "Your $250 contribution helped us print 5,000 voter guides for the Jefferson County doors this weekend."
3. **Match the channel to the audience.** Donors over 60 prefer letters. Volunteers under 35 prefer texts. Reporters prefer email with a clear subject line.
4. **Respond within 24 hours.** Every unanswered message is a lost relationship.
5. **Every communication has one clear ask.** Do not bundle requests.

## Donor Correspondence

### Thank-You (Send within 48 hours of every contribution)

**Email:**
Subject: Thank you, [First Name]

[First Name],

Thank you for your [dollar amount] contribution to our campaign. Your support came at exactly the right time — we are [specific use: launching our field program / hitting the airwaves / expanding our canvass operation] this week, and your generosity makes that possible.

I remember [personal detail if available: our conversation at the town hall / your note about education]. Supporters like you are why we are in this race.

With gratitude,
[Candidate First Name]

**Handwritten note (for donations over $250):**
[First Name] — Your generous support means more than you know. [One sentence connecting their contribution to a specific campaign milestone.] I look forward to making you proud. — [Candidate]

**Text (for online/small-dollar donors):**
[Candidate First Name] here. Just saw your donation come in — thank you! Every dollar fuels our grassroots operation. We won't forget it.

### Donation Ask

**Email:**
Subject: I need your help before [deadline]

[First Name],

I will be direct: [specific reason for the ask — filing deadline, ad buy opportunity, matching gift, opponent just raised X]. We need to raise [amount] by [date] to [specific outcome].

Can I count on you for [specific dollar amount]? [Link]

Here is what your contribution makes possible: [one concrete impact statement].

Thank you for considering this. I would not ask if it were not important.

[Candidate]

**Phone script (for call time):**
"Hi [Name], this is [Candidate]. I hope I'm not catching you at a bad time. [Brief personal connection.] I'm calling because [specific reason]. We need to raise [amount] by [date] for [purpose]. Would you be willing to contribute [specific ask amount]? ... That's incredibly generous, thank you. My finance director [Name] will follow up with the details."

### Donor Update (Monthly or after milestones)

**Email:**
Subject: Where your investment is going — [Month] update

[First Name],

You invested in this campaign, and you deserve to know how it is going. Here is our [month] update:

- **Field:** [X doors knocked, Y voter contacts made]
- **Fundraising:** [On track / ahead / behind goal — be honest]
- **Key moment:** [One highlight — endorsement, event, media hit]
- **Coming up:** [What is next and why it matters]

Your support makes all of this real. Thank you for standing with us.

[Candidate]

## Endorser Correspondence

### The Endorsement Ask

**In-person (preferred for high-value endorsers):**
"I respect your voice in this community, and I am asking for your endorsement because [specific reason their endorsement matters]. I believe we share a commitment to [shared value/issue]. Your support would [specific impact: open doors with X constituency, signal credibility on Y issue]. I do not take this lightly, and I want to earn it. What questions can I answer?"

**Written request (for organizational endorsements):**
Subject: Endorsement consideration — [Candidate Name] for [Office]

Dear [Name/Board],

I am writing to respectfully request [Organization]'s endorsement in the [race] election. [Organization] has been a leading voice on [issue], and my platform reflects the same priorities: [2-3 specific policy alignments].

I have enclosed my completed questionnaire and would welcome the opportunity to appear before your board/membership. My record includes [1-2 relevant accomplishments].

I would be honored to earn your support and to be a partner in advancing [shared goal] from [office].

Respectfully,
[Candidate]

### Endorsement Announcement

**Joint press statement:**
"[Endorser name and title] today announced their endorsement of [Candidate] for [Office]. '[Quote from endorser explaining why — 2 sentences, personal and specific.]' [Candidate] said: '[Quote expressing gratitude and shared commitment — 1-2 sentences.]'"

### Endorser Thank-You

**Personal note (within 24 hours of public endorsement):**
[Name] — Your endorsement means a great deal to me and to this campaign. I know you put your own credibility on the line when you support a candidate, and I intend to make you proud. Thank you for believing in what we are building. — [Candidate]

## Voter Correspondence

### Introduction Letter (for targeted mail or door drops)

Dear Neighbor,

My name is [Candidate], and I am running for [Office] because [one sentence motivation]. I am [brief bio: profession, family, community connection].

I believe [district/community] deserves [core promise]. That is why I am focused on [three priorities in brief].

I would love to hear what matters most to you. Reach me at [email/phone] or come to [upcoming event with date and location].

Respectfully,
[Candidate]

### Issue Response Letter (when voters write in about a specific concern)

Dear [Name],

Thank you for writing to me about [issue]. I appreciate you taking the time — it tells me this matters to you, and it matters to me too.

Here is where I stand: [2-3 sentence position, specific and honest]. [If applicable: Here is what I have already done on this issue.] [If there is disagreement: I respect that we may see this differently, and I want you to know I have listened carefully to your perspective.]

I hope to earn your support, and I welcome continuing this conversation.

Sincerely,
[Candidate]

## Volunteer Correspondence

### Recruitment

**Text:**
Hi [Name]! This is [Organizer] from the [Candidate] campaign. We are looking for [specific role: door knockers / phone bankers / event hosts] this [day]. Any chance you could give us [time commitment]? It makes a real difference. [Sign-up link]

**Email:**
Subject: We need you on [date] — [specific activity]

[Name],

Our team is [specific activity] on [date], and we need [number] more people to hit our goal of [specific target]. Can you join us?

When: [Date, time]
Where: [Location]
What you will do: [Brief, honest description]
What to bring: [Anything needed]

No experience necessary — we will train you and pair you with a buddy. [Sign-up link]

### Volunteer Thank-You (Send same day)

**Text:**
[Name] — you were incredible today. [Specific compliment: "You knocked 47 doors!" or "That voter you convinced to come to the town hall was amazing."] Thank you. This campaign runs on people like you.

## Reporter Correspondence

### Story Pitch

**Email:**
Subject: [Newsworthy hook — be specific, not vague]

[Reporter first name],

[One sentence hook that explains why this is a story now.] [One sentence with the key fact or data point.] [One sentence explaining why their readers/viewers/listeners care.]

[Candidate] is available for an interview [timeframe]. I can also connect you with [relevant source: voter, endorser, expert].

[Attached: relevant background document, high-res photo if applicable]

[Comms Director name, phone, email]

### Media Response (when a reporter has called with questions)

Respond within 2 hours. If you need more time, say so: "Thank you for reaching out. We want to give you a thorough response. Can we get back to you by [specific time]?"

Provide: a quotable statement (2-3 sentences, written to be used as-is), background context (not for direct quote), and an offer for a follow-up interview if warranted.

### Media Advisory (for events)

Subject: MEDIA ADVISORY — [Event name], [Date]

**WHAT:** [One sentence description]
**WHO:** [Candidate, notable attendees]
**WHEN:** [Date, time, doors open vs. program start]
**WHERE:** [Full address, parking/entrance instructions]
**VISUALS:** [What cameras will see — crowd, signage, backdrop]
**RSVP:** [Contact name, phone, email]

## Party Leader Correspondence

### Introduction

**Email or letter:**

Dear [Title] [Last Name],

I am [Candidate], and I am running for [Office] in [jurisdiction]. I wanted to introduce myself and share my vision for this race.

[One paragraph: background and why you are running.]
[One paragraph: how you plan to win — briefly — and how you complement the party's overall strategy.]

I would value the opportunity to meet with you and hear your perspective on the district and the race. I am available [dates/times] and happy to come to you.

Respectfully,
[Candidate]

### Support Request

Be specific about what you need: an introduction, a fundraising co-host, staff recommendation, strategic advice, or an endorsement. Do not make a vague ask. "I would appreciate any support" is weak. "Would you be willing to co-host a reception on [date] and invite your network?" is strong.

## Opponent Correspondence

### Debate Invitation

**Formal letter (also released publicly):**

Dear [Opponent],

The voters of [district] deserve to hear directly from both candidates on the issues that matter to their families. I am writing to propose [number] public debates before [election date].

I propose the following format: [format suggestion]. I am open to a mutually agreed-upon moderator and am flexible on dates and locations.

I have sent this same invitation to [media outlet / civic organization] to serve as a potential host. I look forward to your response.

Respectfully,
[Candidate]

## Tone Guidance by Audience

| Audience | Tone | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Donors | Grateful, transparent, urgent when appropriate | Guilt, desperation, entitlement |
| Endorsers | Respectful, specific, professional | Pressure, assumptions, casual tone |
| Voters | Warm, direct, authentic | Jargon, condescension, vagueness |
| Volunteers | Appreciative, energetic, specific | Taking them for granted, generic praise |
| Reporters | Professional, newsworthy, concise | Spin, burying the lead, being unavailable |
| Party leaders | Respectful, strategic, brief | Over-familiarity, lecturing, desperation |
| Opponents | Formal, public-facing, dignified | Personal attacks, sarcasm, provocation |

## Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**
- Personalize every communication where possible
- Proofread everything — typos in fundraising emails cost money
- Keep a correspondence log so no one falls through the cracks
- Let the candidate's voice come through, even in staff-drafted messages
- Follow up when you say you will

**Don't:**
- Send mass emails that read like mass emails
- Bury your ask at the bottom
- Use all caps, excessive exclamation points, or clickbait subject lines
- Cc people who should be Bcc'd (especially donor lists)
- Send anything when angry — draft it, wait an hour, revise
- Forget that every email can be forwarded and every letter can be photographed
