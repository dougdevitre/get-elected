# Campaign Documents: Master Template Reference

The index and structural guide for every document type a campaign produces. For each category: purpose, audience, tone, structure, and length guidance. Use these as starting frameworks -- adapt to your candidate, race, and moment.

---

## Speeches

### Stump Speech
The candidate's standard campaign speech, delivered at rallies, community events, and public appearances. The foundation of the public message. **Audience:** General voters, supporters, media. **Tone:** Inspirational, personal, conversational. **Structure:** Opening hook (personal story or local reference, 30 sec) > Why I am running (1 min) > Three-pillar platform (1-2 min each with story or data) > The contrast (1 min) > Call to action (30 sec) > Close that echoes the opening (30 sec). **Length:** 7-10 minutes standard, 3-5 short version, 15-20 keynote.

### Announcement Speech
The candidate's formal declaration of candidacy. Sets the tone for the entire campaign. **Audience:** Media, supporters, community, potential donors. **Tone:** Bold, personal, forward-looking. **Structure:** Personal story and motivation > The problem this community faces > Why I am the person to solve it > 3 signature commitments > Call to action (join, donate, volunteer) > Aspirational close. **Length:** 12-18 minutes. This is the one speech that can run longer.

### Victory Speech
Delivered on election night after a win. Defines the candidate's transition from campaigner to officeholder. **Audience:** Supporters, media, the entire community (including those who did not vote for you). **Tone:** Humble, grateful, unifying, forward-looking. **Structure:** Thank voters > Thank opponent and call for unity > Thank campaign team, volunteers, staff by name > Thank family > Restate core vision > First priorities in office > Aspirational close. **Length:** 8-12 minutes.

### Concession Speech
Delivered on election night after a loss. Defines the candidate's character and legacy. **Audience:** Supporters, media, the community. **Tone:** Gracious, dignified, brief, encouraging. **Structure:** Acknowledge result clearly > Congratulate opponent by name > Thank supporters, staff, family > Express pride in the campaign's values > Encourage continued civic engagement > Graceful, hopeful close. **Length:** 5-8 minutes. Brevity is a virtue here.

## Written Communications

### Press Release
Formal announcement to media for endorsements, policy launches, events, milestones, and responses. **Audience:** Reporters, editors, news producers. **Tone:** Factual, newsy, quotable. Inverted pyramid (most important first). **Structure:** FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE + date > Active-voice headline > Dateline + lead paragraph (who/what/when/where/why) > Candidate quote > Context paragraph > Supporting quote (endorser/supporter) > Boilerplate (bio, election date, website) > Media contact. **Length:** 300-500 words, one page preferred.

### Op-Ed
Opinion piece under the candidate's byline for newspapers or online publications. Establishes thought leadership on a key issue. **Audience:** Newspaper readers, opinion followers, engaged voters. **Tone:** Personal, authoritative, persuasive. Written in the candidate's voice. **Structure:** Hook (current event, personal experience, or provocative observation) > Thesis > 3 supporting arguments with facts and stories > Preemptive rebuttal of strongest counterargument > Call to action or vision > Close echoing the opening. **Length:** 600-800 words (check publication guidelines).

### Letter to the Editor (LTE)
Short public statement responding to news coverage or raising a campaign issue. Can be written by the candidate, supporters, or endorsers for amplification. **Audience:** Newspaper readers, community members. **Tone:** Concise, clear, personal. **Structure:** Reference the article or issue > State your position > Support with one fact or personal experience > Close with forward-looking statement. **Length:** 150-250 words.

### Questionnaire Responses
Answers to endorsement questionnaires from newspapers, advocacy organizations, and interest groups. Often the deciding factor for endorsements. **Audience:** Editorial boards, organization leaders, screening committees. **Tone:** Substantive, specific, honest. Do not be evasive. **Structure:** Answer each question directly in 2-5 sentences. Lead with your position, then support it. Reference local data or personal experience. **Length:** Varies by questionnaire. Be thorough but not verbose.

## Fundraising Documents

### Call Time Script
Structured script for the candidate's fundraising phone calls. The single most important fundraising tool. **Audience:** The candidate (internal). **Tone:** Brief, personal, direct. **Structure per call:** Personal greeting and connection > Brief campaign update or urgency > The ask (specific dollar amount) > Handle response (pledged/declined/think about it) > Thank and close. Each call sheet line includes: name, phone, relationship, giving history, personal notes, suggested ask amount, outcome field. **Length:** Script itself is half a page. Call sheets are one prospect per row.

### Ask Email / Fundraising Email
Email solicitation for online donations. The workhorse of small-dollar fundraising. **Audience:** Donor list (prospects and existing). **Tone:** Urgent but not panicked, personal, direct. The candidate is talking to you, not at you. **Structure:** Subject line (specific, intriguing, or urgent) > Personal opening (1 sentence) > Why now (deadline, opponent action, milestone, match) > Impact (what the money will do specifically) > The ask (specific amount + donate link) > P.S. line (restates the ask -- many readers skip to the P.S.). **Length:** 200-400 words. Shorter for deadline reminders.

### Thank-You Letter
Post-donation acknowledgment. Legally required for tax purposes and strategically critical for donor retention. **Audience:** Donors at all levels. **Tone:** Warm, grateful, personal. **Structure:** Thank the donor by name > Reference the specific amount > State what the money will fund > Reaffirm the campaign's mission > Invite continued engagement (event, volunteer, share). Handwritten notes for major donors; personalized email for all others. **Length:** 3-5 sentences for email; 2-3 sentences for handwritten note.

### Event Materials (Fundraising)
Invitations, programs, and collateral for fundraising events. **Audience:** Prospective donors and supporters. **Tone:** Warm, exclusive, action-oriented. **Structure:** Host name(s) + "invite you to" > Event type and candidate name > Date, time, location > Contribution levels (tiered: host, sponsor, guest) > RSVP contact > Legal disclaimer (paid for by, required disclosures). **Length:** One page or single email.

## Voter Contact Documents

### Door Knock Script (Canvass Script)
Guide for volunteers knocking on doors. Must be natural, not robotic. **Audience:** Campaign volunteers (internal tool). **Tone:** Conversational, warm, brief. Scripts are frameworks, not word-for-word recitations. **Structure:** Introduction (who you are, why you are here -- 2 sentences) > Voter ID question ("Have you thought about who you are supporting?") > If supportive/undecided: positive message (2-3 sentences) > The ask (vote commitment, volunteer, yard sign) > Thank and close. If not supportive: thank them, move on. Never argue. **Length:** Half a page. Readable at a glance while standing at a door.

### Phone Script
Guide for phone bank volunteers. Shorter and faster-paced than door scripts. **Audience:** Volunteers (internal). **Tone:** Friendly, brief. **Structure:** Introduction + ask for the right person (2 sentences) > Voter ID question > Brief message (1-2 sentences) > Ask (vote commitment or event attendance) > Thank and close. **Length:** Quarter page. Volunteers should spend 90% of the call listening.

### Text Script
Templates for peer-to-peer or broadcast text campaigns. **Audience:** Voters via volunteer texters or campaign platform. **Tone:** Brief, personal, mobile-native. No formal language. **Structure:** One message per text: who you are, why you are texting, the ask, a link if needed. **Length:** 160 characters for SMS, up to 300 for MMS with image.

### Walk Card / Palm Card
The physical leave-behind for door-to-door canvassing. What the voter looks at after the canvasser leaves. **Audience:** Targeted voters. **Tone:** Clean, simple, action-oriented. **Structure:** Front: candidate photo, name, office, election date, tagline. Back: 3 key priorities (one line each), endorsement logos, website, social media. **Length:** 4x6 or 5x7 card. Every word must earn its space.

## Digital Documents

### Social Media Posts
Platform-specific content for Facebook, Instagram, X/Twitter, TikTok, and other channels. **Audience:** Followers and targeted voters. **Tone:** Varies by platform -- Facebook (conversational, community), Instagram (visual, aspirational), X (punchy, newsy), TikTok (authentic, informal). **Structure:** Hook (first line grabs attention) > Message (1-3 sentences) > Call to action (link, share, comment) > Hashtags (2-5, CamelCase for accessibility). **Length:** Facebook 40-80 words, X under 280 characters, Instagram caption 20-50 words, TikTok caption under 30 words.

### Email Sequences
Multi-email series for onboarding new supporters, fundraising pushes, or GOTV mobilization. **Audience:** Segmented email list. **Tone:** Builds from introduction to urgency over the sequence. **Structure:** Email 1 (welcome/introduction) > Email 2 (the stakes/story) > Email 3 (the ask) > Email 4 (deadline/urgency) > Email 5 (last chance). Each email stands alone but builds on the previous. **Length:** 150-400 words per email.

### Website Copy
The campaign's central information hub. **Audience:** Voters, donors, volunteers, media. **Tone:** Professional, welcoming, action-oriented. **Key pages:** Home (hero image, tagline, 3 CTAs), About (bio 400-600 words), Issues (one page per issue, 200-400 words each), Endorsements (by category with quotes), Events (calendar with sign-ups), Donate (simple mobile-friendly form), Volunteer (sign-up with shift options), Contact, Media (press releases, press kit, high-res photos). **Length:** Varies by page.

### Ad Copy (Digital and Mail)
Paid advertising content for digital platforms and direct mail. **Audience:** Targeted voter segments. **Tone:** Varies by purpose -- positive bio, issue contrast, GOTV urgency. **Structure (positive):** Candidate image + name + tagline + 1-2 key promises + CTA. **Structure (contrast):** Split image or side-by-side + "[Candidate] vs. [Opponent]" on 2-3 issues with citations + "The choice is clear." **Length:** Digital: 15-40 words primary text, 5-8 word headline. Mail: front image + headline, inside 50-100 words, back endorsements + election info.

## Compliance Documents

### Disclaimer Language
Legal disclaimers required on all campaign communications. "Paid for by [Committee Name]" at minimum. Many jurisdictions add treasurer name, authorization language, or other requirements. Required on every printed piece, email, digital ad, yard sign, and website. Check jurisdiction-specific rules for size, font, placement, and exact language.

### Treasurer Memos
Internal memos from the treasurer documenting compliance decisions, contribution reviews, and expenditure approvals. **Audience:** Campaign manager, candidate, legal counsel (internal, confidential). **Tone:** Factual, precise, documented. **Length:** 1-2 pages per memo.

### Report Checklists
Step-by-step checklists for preparing campaign finance filings. See `compliance-report-prep.md` for the full process. **Audience:** Treasurer, compliance staff (internal). **Structure:** Pre-filing data review > Contribution verification > Expenditure categorization > Balance reconciliation > Filing submission > Confirmation documentation. **Length:** 1-2 pages.

## Strategy Documents

### Campaign Plan
The comprehensive strategic document guiding the entire campaign. **Audience:** Campaign manager, senior staff, candidate (internal, confidential). **Tone:** Analytical, strategic, honest. **Structure:** Race overview > Path to victory (vote goal, targets, win number) > Candidate SWOT > Opponent assessment > Message framework > Voter targeting > Field plan > Communications plan > Fundraising plan > Budget > Timeline. **Length:** 15-30 pages. See `campaign-plan-builder.md`.

### Budget
The financial blueprint for the campaign. **Audience:** Campaign manager, treasurer, candidate (internal). **Structure:** Revenue projections by source and month > Expenditure plan by category and month > Cash flow projections > Reserve requirements > Variance tracking. **Length:** Spreadsheet format, 2-5 pages summary.

### Targeting Plan
The data-driven voter contact strategy. **Audience:** Campaign manager, field director, data team (internal). **Structure:** Win number > Turnout model > Universe definition by tier > Precinct-level targets > Contact mode assignments > Timeline. **Length:** 5-10 pages. See `voter-targeting.md`.

### GOTV Plan
The final-stretch voter mobilization strategy. **Audience:** Field director, campaign manager (internal). **Structure:** Chase universe > Early vote program > Absentee chase sequence > Election Day operations > Staffing and volunteer deployment > Communication chain. **Length:** 5-10 pages. See `gotv-plan.md` and `ballot-chase-program.md`.

## Event Documents

### Run of Show
Minute-by-minute timeline for complex events (rallies, fundraisers, launches). **Audience:** Event staff, AV team, volunteers (internal). **Tone:** Precise, sequential, no ambiguity. **Structure:** Time > Action > Responsible person > Notes. Includes setup, doors open, program start, each speaker/segment with duration, program end, teardown. **Length:** 1-2 pages.

### Briefing Memo
Prepares the candidate for every event. See `scheduling-advance.md` for the full template. **Audience:** Candidate (internal). **Tone:** Brief, practical, actionable. Covers event details, purpose, audience, host, key people, talking points, the ask, sensitive topics, entry/exit plan. **Length:** 1 page per event.

### Advance Memo
Operational guide for the advance staff person managing event logistics. **Audience:** Advance person, event volunteers (internal). **Tone:** Detailed, logistical. Covers venue layout, AV setup, parking, signage placement, volunteer assignments, timeline, contingency plans, vendor contacts. **Length:** 1-2 pages. See `scheduling-advance.md`.

## Document Management Rules

1. **Version control:** Every document has a date in the filename or header. Never edit without updating the date.
2. **Confidentiality:** Mark internal documents CONFIDENTIAL. Opposition research is HIGHLY CONFIDENTIAL.
3. **Approval chain:** Press releases, paid media, and policy statements require candidate or campaign manager approval before distribution.
4. **Centralized storage:** All documents in a backed-up, access-controlled location with consistent folder structure.
5. **Legal hold:** Preserve all campaign documents for the period required by your jurisdiction (typically 3-5 years post-election).
