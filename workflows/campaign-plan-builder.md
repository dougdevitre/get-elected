# Campaign Plan Builder

A comprehensive template for building your campaign plan document. This is the strategic blueprint that guides every decision, dollar, and hour of your campaign. A good campaign plan is written early, referenced constantly, and updated as conditions change.

---

## How to Use This Template

Work through each section in order. The campaign plan should be a single document (15-30 pages) that your core team has read and internalized. Do not skip sections -- even if a section seems less relevant, the discipline of thinking through it will pay off.

---

## Section 1: Executive Summary

Write this last, but place it first. One page maximum.

- [ ] State the office you are seeking and election date
- [ ] Name the candidate and 2-sentence biography
- [ ] State your theory of victory in 1-2 sentences
- [ ] State your win number (vote goal)
- [ ] State your total budget and fundraising goal
- [ ] Summarize your path: what must go right for you to win
- [ ] Identify the single biggest risk to your campaign

**Template:**

> [Candidate Name] is running for [Office] in the [Date] election. The win number is [X] votes based on projected turnout of [Y]. The campaign will raise $[Z] and execute a [voter contact strategy] focused on [target universe]. Victory depends on [theory of victory summary].

## Section 2: Race Overview

### District Profile
- [ ] Geography: boundaries, communities, urban/suburban/rural mix
- [ ] Population and registered voter count
- [ ] Partisan registration breakdown (D / R / I / Other)
- [ ] Key demographic data: age, race/ethnicity, income, education
- [ ] Major employers, institutions, and community anchors
- [ ] Media market(s) covering the district

### Electoral History
- [ ] Results for this office in the last 3-4 cycles
- [ ] Turnout for comparable elections (same office, same cycle type)
- [ ] Precinct-level performance data: strongest and weakest areas by party
- [ ] Any notable trends (shifting demographics, turnout changes)

### Opponent Assessment
- [ ] Incumbent or open seat
- [ ] Known or likely opponents and their strengths/weaknesses
- [ ] Opponents' likely fundraising capacity
- [ ] Opponents' likely base of support
- [ ] Opponents' vulnerabilities (see `opposition-research.md`)

---

## Section 3: Theory of Victory

Your theory of victory is the narrative explanation of how you win. It should be specific enough to be falsifiable.

- [ ] Define your coalition: which voter groups combine to reach your win number
- [ ] Identify the key battleground: is this a base-mobilization or persuasion race?
- [ ] State the conditions that must exist on election day for you to win
- [ ] Identify what changes from prior elections that makes your victory possible
- [ ] Articulate why your candidacy is the right fit for this moment

**Example theories of victory:**

- "We win by turning out low-propensity Democratic voters in precincts 4, 7, and 12 while holding our share of independents in the suburban precincts."
- "We win by persuading moderate suburban voters who supported the incumbent last time but are dissatisfied with [issue], while maintaining base turnout."

---

## Section 4: Vote Goal Calculation

### The Win Number Formula

```
Expected Turnout = [Average turnout from last 3 comparable elections]
Win Number = (Expected Turnout / 2) + 1 + Safety Margin
Safety Margin = Expected Turnout x 0.03 to 0.05 (3-5%)
```

- [ ] Research turnout from last 3 comparable elections
- [ ] Calculate average expected turnout
- [ ] Calculate raw win number: (expected turnout / 2) + 1
- [ ] Add safety margin (3-5% of expected turnout)
- [ ] Final win number = raw win number + safety margin
- [ ] Adjust for multi-candidate races (win number may be lower in a 3+ way race)

### Targeting Breakdown
- [ ] Base voters (reliable supporters): estimated count
- [ ] Persuadable voters (swing/independent): estimated count needed
- [ ] Mobilization targets (low-propensity supporters): estimated count needed
- [ ] Total voter contact universe = base + persuadable + mobilization targets
- [ ] Required contact rate to hit win number (typically 2-3x your persuasion target)

## Section 5: Targeting Strategy

Define who you are talking to and in what priority order. See `voter-targeting.md` for detailed methodology.

- [ ] Tier 1 (highest priority): base voters who need mobilization
- [ ] Tier 2: persuadable voters in key precincts
- [ ] Tier 3: low-propensity supporters for GOTV push
- [ ] Geographic priorities: rank precincts by vote potential
- [ ] Demographic priorities: identify key demographic segments
- [ ] Issue-based targeting: voters motivated by your core issues

## Section 6: Budget Framework

### Budget Categories (Typical Allocation)

| Category | Small Local Race | Mid-Size Race | Large/Federal Race |
|---|---|---|---|
| Staff/Consultants | 10-15% | 20-30% | 25-35% |
| Paid Media (TV/Radio/Digital) | 0-10% | 30-40% | 40-50% |
| Direct Mail | 20-30% | 15-25% | 10-15% |
| Field (doors, phones, GOTV) | 15-25% | 10-15% | 10-15% |
| Fundraising Costs | 10-15% | 10-15% | 10-15% |
| Signs/Printed Materials | 10-15% | 3-5% | 1-3% |
| Events | 5-10% | 3-5% | 2-5% |
| Office/Operations | 5-10% | 5-10% | 5-10% |
| Reserve/Contingency | 5-10% | 5-10% | 5-10% |

- [ ] Set total budget based on comparable race research
- [ ] Allocate budget by category using the framework above
- [ ] Build a monthly cash flow projection (income vs. expenses)
- [ ] Identify the minimum viable budget (what you absolutely must spend)
- [ ] Plan for a late-campaign reserve (at least 10% of budget for final push)
- [ ] Track burn rate weekly once the campaign is underway

## Section 7: Campaign Timeline

### Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-2)
- Filing, compliance, infrastructure, early fundraising (see `first-30-days.md`)

### Phase 2: Building (Months 3-5)
- Ramp up fundraising, begin voter contact, build volunteer base, earn media

### Phase 3: Persuasion (Months 6-8 or final 90 days)
- Paid media launch, intensify voter contact, debates/forums, endorsement push

### Phase 4: Mobilization / GOTV (Final 30 days)
- Early vote chase, final mail drops, GOTV operations (see `gotv-plan.md`)

- [ ] Map all key dates (filing, primary, general, early voting, debates)
- [ ] Set monthly milestones for fundraising, voter contact, volunteer recruitment
- [ ] Build in decision points (when to adjust strategy based on data)

## Section 9: Message Framework

### Core Message (The "Why")
- [ ] One-sentence message: "I am running because..."
- [ ] 30-second elevator pitch
- [ ] 2-minute stump speech framework
- [ ] Positive vision statement (what you are FOR, not just against)

### Issue Positions
- [ ] Top 3 issues with 1-paragraph positions
- [ ] Supporting issues (5-7 additional positions)
- [ ] "Bridge" language connecting issues to your core narrative

### Contrast Framework
- [ ] Identify 2-3 areas of clear contrast with your opponent
- [ ] Frame contrasts around values and priorities, not personal attacks
- [ ] Prepare response lines for your known vulnerabilities

### Earned Media Strategy
- [ ] Key media contacts (reporters, editors, editorial boards)
- [ ] Press release calendar tied to campaign milestones
- [ ] Letter-to-the-editor campaign plan
- [ ] Candidate op-ed schedule

### Paid Media Strategy
- [ ] Determine paid media mix (digital, mail, TV, radio, print)
- [ ] Set media budget and timeline
- [ ] Define target audiences for paid media
- [ ] Plan creative production timeline
- [ ] Include required disclaimers on all paid communications

### Digital/Social Media Plan
- [ ] Content calendar framework (daily/weekly posting schedule)
- [ ] Paid social media advertising plan and budget
- [ ] Email communication schedule
- [ ] Video content plan

## Section 11: Field Plan

### Voter Contact Goals
- [ ] Total doors to knock (typically 3-5x your persuasion target)
- [ ] Total phone calls to make
- [ ] Shifts needed to hit goals (calculate: doors per shift x shifts needed)
- [ ] Volunteer recruitment targets to staff those shifts

### Canvassing Operations
- [ ] Turf cutting plan (geographic areas for each canvass shift)
- [ ] Script development for door and phone
- [ ] Data collection and entry process
- [ ] Canvass launch date and weekly schedule

## Section 12: GOTV Plan

Summarize your get-out-the-vote strategy here. See `gotv-plan.md` for full operational detail.

- [ ] Early vote / absentee chase plan
- [ ] Election day operations plan
- [ ] Volunteer deployment schedule for final 4 days
- [ ] Poll monitoring plan
- [ ] Ride-to-polls coordination

---

## Finalizing the Plan

- [ ] Draft all sections and compile into a single document
- [ ] Review with campaign manager and key advisors
- [ ] Share with core team (treasurer, field director, communications)
- [ ] Set a date to revisit and update (monthly or after major developments)
- [ ] Keep the plan confidential -- it contains strategic information

The campaign plan is a living document. Review it monthly, but do not change strategy based on a single data point. Consistent execution of a good plan beats constant pivots every time.
