# Filing Checklist

A universal checklist for filing your candidacy and establishing your campaign as a legal entity. These are the non-negotiable first steps before any public campaign activity, fundraising, or spending can begin.

---

> **EDUCATIONAL DISCLAIMER:** Filing requirements vary significantly across jurisdictions -- federal, state, county, and municipal races each have their own rules, forms, deadlines, and regulatory bodies. This checklist provides general best practices applicable to most U.S. campaigns. Always verify the specific requirements for your race with your local elections office, secretary of state, or the Federal Election Commission (FEC). Consider consulting a campaign finance attorney. This guide is for educational purposes and does not constitute legal advice.

---

## Step 1: Determine Your Filing Requirements

Before you file anything, research the specific requirements for your race.

- [ ] Identify the exact office you are running for (title, jurisdiction, district)
- [ ] Identify the governing election authority (e.g., county clerk, secretary of state, FEC)
- [ ] Obtain the official candidate filing packet or instructions
- [ ] Determine the filing period (opening and closing dates)
- [ ] Determine whether you need to file by petition, fee, or both
- [ ] If petition signatures are required, determine the number needed and deadline
- [ ] Identify any eligibility requirements (age, residency, citizenship, party affiliation)
- [ ] Determine if a primary election applies and its date
- [ ] Identify the campaign finance regulatory body (may differ from elections office)
- [ ] Obtain the campaign finance filing calendar (all report due dates)
- [ ] Determine contribution limits for your specific race
- [ ] Identify prohibited contribution sources for your jurisdiction
- [ ] Research personal financial disclosure requirements

## Step 2: Gather Required Documents

Collect all documents you may need before visiting the elections office.

- [ ] Government-issued photo ID
- [ ] Proof of residency (utility bill, lease, mortgage statement, voter registration)
- [ ] Birth certificate or proof of citizenship (if required for the office)
- [ ] Voter registration confirmation
- [ ] Social Security number (needed for EIN application and some filings)
- [ ] Filing fee (check, money order, or other accepted payment)
- [ ] Petition forms (if applicable -- obtain from elections office)
- [ ] Party affiliation documentation (if applicable)
- [ ] Completed candidate questionnaire or application (if provided by elections office)
- [ ] Financial disclosure forms (if required at time of filing)

---

## Step 3: File Statement of Candidacy

This is the official declaration that you are running for office.

- [ ] Complete the Statement of Candidacy (or equivalent form for your jurisdiction)
- [ ] Double-check all information for accuracy (name as it appears on ballot, address, office)
- [ ] Determine how your name will appear on the ballot and confirm it is correct
- [ ] Submit the form to the appropriate elections office
- [ ] Pay the filing fee (retain the receipt)
- [ ] Submit petition signatures if required (keep copies of all pages)
- [ ] Obtain a stamped or signed copy of your filed documents
- [ ] Record your candidate ID number if one is assigned
- [ ] Note any additional deadlines triggered by filing (e.g., financial disclosure due dates)
- [ ] Confirm with the elections office that your filing is complete and accepted

### Common Pitfalls
- Filing outside the filing window (too early or too late)
- Name on filing does not match voter registration
- Insufficient petition signatures or signatures from outside the district
- Missing a required supplemental form
- Not meeting residency requirements as of the required date

---

## Step 4: Register Your Campaign Committee

Most jurisdictions require you to register a formal campaign committee.

- [ ] Choose a committee name (common formats: "Friends of [Name]," "[Name] for [Office]," "[Name] [Year]")
- [ ] Verify the name is not already in use with the regulatory body
- [ ] Complete the Statement of Organization (or committee registration form)
- [ ] Designate the committee as your principal campaign committee
- [ ] List the candidate name, office sought, and election
- [ ] Designate a campaign treasurer (see Step 6)
- [ ] Provide the committee's mailing address
- [ ] File the registration with the appropriate campaign finance regulatory body
- [ ] Obtain your committee ID number and confirmation of registration
- [ ] Retain copies of all filed documents

---

## Step 5: Obtain an Employer Identification Number (EIN)

Your campaign committee needs its own EIN for banking and tax purposes.

- [ ] Apply for an EIN from the IRS (free, can be done online at irs.gov/ein)
- [ ] Use the committee name as the legal name
- [ ] Select the appropriate entity type (typically "Other" or "Political Organization")
- [ ] Record the EIN securely -- you will need it for the bank account and filings
- [ ] Do not use your personal Social Security number for campaign finances

---

## Step 6: Appoint a Campaign Treasurer

> **EDUCATIONAL DISCLAIMER:** Treasurer responsibilities and requirements vary by jurisdiction. In some jurisdictions, the candidate may serve as their own treasurer; in others, a separate individual is required. The treasurer role carries personal legal liability in many jurisdictions. Ensure your treasurer understands their obligations. See `treasurer-setup.md` for detailed guidance.

- [ ] Select a treasurer who is detail-oriented, trustworthy, and available
- [ ] Confirm the person is eligible to serve as treasurer in your jurisdiction
- [ ] Brief them on their responsibilities (record-keeping, reporting, compliance)
- [ ] File the treasurer designation with the campaign finance regulatory body
- [ ] Provide the treasurer with the filing calendar and all regulatory guidance
- [ ] Establish a working relationship: how often will you meet? Who has signing authority?
- [ ] Consider designating a deputy or assistant treasurer as a backup

---

## Step 7: Open a Campaign Bank Account

- [ ] Bring to the bank: EIN confirmation letter, committee registration, treasurer ID, candidate ID
- [ ] Open a checking account in the committee's legal name
- [ ] Determine signatory requirements (treasurer alone, or treasurer + candidate)
- [ ] Order checks printed with the committee name and address
- [ ] Set up online banking for easy monitoring
- [ ] Do NOT commingle personal funds with campaign funds
- [ ] Record the account number securely
- [ ] Deposit any initial seed money (personal contribution from the candidate, if applicable)
- [ ] Note: some banks are unfamiliar with political committee accounts -- call ahead

---

## Step 8: File Initial Campaign Finance Report

Many jurisdictions require an initial report shortly after committee registration.

- [ ] Determine if an initial report is required and its due date
- [ ] Report any contributions received (including candidate seed money)
- [ ] Report any expenditures made (including filing fees if paid from campaign funds)
- [ ] Ensure the report format matches your regulatory body's requirements
- [ ] File electronically if available (preferred for speed and accuracy)
- [ ] Retain confirmation of filing
- [ ] Mark all future filing deadlines on your compliance calendar

---

## Step 9: Set Up Record-Keeping Systems

Good records from day one prevent compliance nightmares later. See `donation-intake.md` and `expenditure-tracking.md` for detailed procedures.

- [ ] Choose a record-keeping system (spreadsheet, campaign finance software, or accounting software)
- [ ] Set up contribution tracking (date, donor name, address, employer, occupation, amount)
- [ ] Set up expenditure tracking (date, payee, amount, purpose, category)
- [ ] Create a physical filing system for receipts, invoices, and correspondence
- [ ] Create a digital backup system (scanned receipts, cloud storage)
- [ ] Establish a process for depositing contributions within the required timeframe
- [ ] Set up a filing calendar with reminders at least 2 weeks before each deadline
- [ ] Brief all staff/volunteers who handle money on proper procedures

## After Filing: Immediate Next Steps

- [ ] Confirm all filings are acknowledged and complete
- [ ] Verify you are on the ballot or qualified for the ballot
- [ ] Set up compliance calendar with all future deadlines
- [ ] Begin fundraising operations (see `fundraising-plan.md`)
- [ ] Proceed to campaign infrastructure buildout (see `first-30-days.md`)

Keep copies of every filed document, every receipt, and every confirmation. If a question ever arises about your filing status or compliance, you want to have the paper trail.
