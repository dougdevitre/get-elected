# Press Release Templates

Standard press release format and ready-to-customize templates for every major campaign moment. A properly formatted press release dramatically increases local media pickup. Follow these templates exactly -- reporters expect this structure.

---

## Standard Press Release Format

Every campaign press release follows this structure:

1. **Header**: "FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE" (or "EMBARGOED UNTIL [date/time]") in all caps
2. **Date**: Full date of release
3. **Contact**: Press contact name, phone, email
4. **Headline**: Bold, present tense, newsworthy (under 15 words)
5. **Subhead** (optional): One line adding context
6. **Dateline**: CITY, STATE -- opening the first paragraph
7. **Lead paragraph**: Who, what, when, where, why in 2-3 sentences
8. **Quote from candidate**: 2-3 sentences in the candidate's voice showing passion or vision
9. **Body**: Supporting details, data, background (1-3 paragraphs)
10. **Second quote** (optional): From endorser, supporter, or campaign official
11. **Boilerplate**: Standard "About [Candidate Name]" paragraph used on every release
12. **End mark**: Three hash marks centered: ###
13. **Editor's note** (optional): Photo availability, interview scheduling info

## Template 1: Campaign Announcement

```
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
[Month Day, Year]

Contact: [Press Secretary Name]
Phone: [Phone] | Email: [Email]

[CANDIDATE FULL NAME] ANNOUNCES CAMPAIGN FOR [OFFICE]

[City], [State] -- [Candidate Full Name] today announced [his/her/their]
candidacy for [Office] in [jurisdiction], pledging to [primary campaign
promise in one clause]. The [primary/general] election will be held on
[Election Date].

"[Insert 2-3 sentence quote expressing WHY the candidate is running.
Focus on community need, not personal ambition. Example: 'Our families
deserve leadership that puts their needs first. I'm running because
[specific local issue] demands action, and I have the experience to
deliver results.']" said [Last Name].

[Candidate Last Name] brings [X years] of experience in [relevant field]
to the race. [He/She/They] previously served as [relevant credential]
and has been recognized for [relevant achievement]. [Add one sentence
on key policy priority.]

[Standard boilerplate -- see below.]

###
```

```
[Candidate Last Name].

matters. Include their title, years of service, or membership numbers
if an organization.]

[Standard boilerplate.]
```


## Template 6: Rapid Response

```
FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE
[Month Day, Year]

Contact: [Press Secretary Name]
Phone: [Phone] | Email: [Email]

[CANDIDATE NAME] RESPONDS TO [BRIEF DESCRIPTION OF NEWS/OPPONENT ACTION]

[City], [State] -- [Candidate Name] today issued the following statement
in response to [specific news event, opponent statement, or policy
development]:

"[Direct, forceful quote. First sentence: state your position clearly.
Second sentence: provide evidence or contrast. Third sentence: pivot to
your vision. Keep the tone firm but not personal. Attack positions, not
people. Example: 'The decision to [action] is wrong for [jurisdiction].
The facts show [counter-evidence]. Our community deserves [your
alternative].']"

[One paragraph of supporting facts, data, or context that backs up
the candidate's statement. Cite specific sources.]

[Standard boilerplate.]

###

NOTE TO CAMPAIGN: Rapid responses must go out within 2-4 hours of the
triggering event. Have pre-approved language on likely attack topics.
Route through legal review only if it references litigation or legal
claims.
```

```
Example: 'Every vote deserves to be counted. We are confident that when
all ballots are tallied, the result will reflect our community's voice.
We will have more to say when the count is complete.']"

[Standard boilerplate.]
```

## Standard Boilerplate (Use on Every Release)

Adapt this paragraph and include it at the bottom of every press release:

```
About [Candidate Full Name]:
[Candidate Name] is a candidate for [Office] in [Jurisdiction]. A
[profession/background], [he/she/they] has [key credential 1] and [key
credential 2]. [Candidate Last Name] lives in [City] with [family
details if candidate chooses to share]. Learn more at [website]. Follow
the campaign on [social handles].
```

## Distribution Tips

- **Build your press list**: Name, outlet, beat, email, phone for every local reporter
- **Send timing**: Tuesday through Thursday, 9:00-10:00 AM local time for maximum pickup
- **Subject line**: Use the headline verbatim as your email subject line
- **Attachments**: Paste the full release in the email body AND attach as a PDF
- **Follow up**: Call your top 3-5 reporters 2 hours after sending
- **Embargoes**: Use sparingly; only when you need coordinated timing (e.g., major endorsement)
- **Photos**: Always offer a high-resolution headshot and event photos if applicable
