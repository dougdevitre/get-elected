# Florida Ballot Access Requirements

> **STALENESS WARNING:** This reference was written in April 2026. Filing deadlines,
> fees, and petition signature requirements may change through legislation. Always verify
> current requirements with the Florida Division of Elections at https://dos.fl.gov/elections/
> before filing.

> **EDUCATIONAL DISCLAIMER:** This document is for educational and informational purposes
> only. It does not constitute legal advice. Campaigns should consult a qualified election
> law attorney or the Division of Elections for guidance specific to their situation.

## Overview

Florida uses a closed primary system for partisan offices. Only voters registered with a
party may vote in that party's primary, with an important exception: if all candidates in
a race are from the same party and no other candidate (including write-ins) has qualified,
the primary is opened to all voters ("universal primary" exception). Florida also has a
notable **resign-to-run law** that affects certain officeholders.

---

## Qualifying Methods

Candidates may qualify for the ballot through **filing fees** or **petition signatures**
(or a combination for some offices).

### Option 1: Filing Fee (Qualifying Fee)

| Office | Filing Fee |
|--------|-----------|
| Governor | 6% of annual salary |
| Lieutenant Governor | 6% of annual salary |
| Cabinet offices (AG, CFO, Commissioner of Agriculture) | 6% of annual salary |
| State Senator | 4% of annual salary |
| State Representative | 4% of annual salary |
| U.S. Senator | 6% of annual salary |
| U.S. Representative | 6% of annual salary |
| County Commissioner | 4-6% of annual salary |
| School Board | 4% of annual salary |
| Other county constitutional officers | 4-6% of annual salary |

In addition to the qualifying fee, candidates pay a **party assessment** (for partisan
races) of 1-2% of the annual salary of the office.

### Option 2: Petition Alternative

Candidates may qualify by petition in lieu of paying the qualifying fee:

| Office | Signatures Required |
|--------|-------------------|
| Statewide offices | 1% of registered voters in the state (party primary) or 1% of total registered voters (NPA) |
| State Senator | 1% of registered voters in the district (party) |
| State Representative | 1% of registered voters in the district (party) |
| County offices | 1% of registered voters in the jurisdiction (party) |

- **Party candidates:** Signatures must be from registered voters of the same party in
  the relevant jurisdiction.
- **No-party-affiliation (NPA) candidates:** Signatures from any registered voter in
  the jurisdiction.
- **Petition filing deadline:** Typically the first Tuesday after the first Monday in
  May of the election year (well before the qualifying period).

### Independent / No-Party-Affiliation (NPA) Candidates

NPA candidates qualify by petition and pay a qualifying fee (or collect additional
petition signatures in lieu of the fee). The petition signature requirement is typically:

| Office | Signatures Required |
|--------|-------------------|
| Statewide offices | 1% of total registered voters in the state |
| Congressional / legislative | 1% of total registered voters in the district |
| County offices | 3% of registered voters in the jurisdiction |

## Resign-to-Run Law

Florida's resign-to-run law (Section 99.012, Florida Statutes) requires certain
officeholders to resign before qualifying for another office:

- **Applies when:** An officeholder seeks an office whose term overlaps with their
  current term (i.e., they cannot serve both simultaneously).
- **Resignation deadline:** Must submit an irrevocable resignation at least 10 days
  before the first day of qualifying for the new office.
- **Exceptions:** Federal officeholders, candidates running for an office with a term
  beginning after their current term expires, and certain other exceptions.
- **Practical impact:** This law forces officeholders to commit before qualifying --
  there is no "test the waters" period while holding office.

---

## Qualifying Period

- **State and multi-county offices:** Qualifying occurs during a one-week period set by
  the Division of Elections (typically in mid-June of the election year).
- **County and local offices:** Qualifying period is noon on the Monday through noon on
  the Friday of the qualifying week.
- Candidates must file all required documents and pay fees during the qualifying period.
  Missing the deadline means no ballot access.

---

## Closed Primary Exception (Universal Primary)

If all candidates for a particular office in the general election are from **one party
only** (no other party candidates or NPA candidates have qualified, including write-ins),
then the primary for that office is **open to all registered voters** regardless of
party. This is the "universal primary" rule.

- This rule applies on a race-by-race basis.
- Write-in candidates can close the primary by qualifying, since their presence means
  not all general election candidates are from one party.

---

## Write-In Candidates

- Write-in candidates must file qualifying papers during the qualifying period.
- **No filing fee** is required for write-in candidates.
- Write-in candidates appear on the general election ballot (not the primary).
- **Strategic use:** Because write-in qualification closes a universal primary, write-in
  candidates are sometimes used strategically to prevent a party primary from being
  opened to all voters.
- Write-in votes are counted only for qualified write-in candidates.

## Residency and Eligibility Requirements

| Office | Minimum Age | Residency Requirement |
|--------|------------|----------------------|
| Governor | 30 | 7 years Florida resident |
| Lieutenant Governor | 30 | 7 years Florida resident |
| Cabinet offices | 30 | 7 years Florida resident |
| State Senator | 21 | 2 years Florida resident, district resident at time of qualifying |
| State Representative | 21 | 2 years Florida resident, district resident at time of qualifying |
| U.S. Senator | 30 | Florida resident, 9 years U.S. citizen |
| U.S. Representative | 25 | Florida resident, 7 years U.S. citizen |
| County Commissioner | 18 | District resident |
| School Board | 18 | District resident |

## Key Dates (General Reference)

| Event | Typical Timing |
|-------|---------------|
| Petition filing deadline | First Tuesday after first Monday in May |
| Qualifying period | Mid-June (one week) |
| Primary election | Fourth Tuesday in August |
| General election | First Tuesday after first Monday in November |

---

## Special Elections

Special elections to fill vacancies are called by the Governor. The qualifying period
and election dates are specified in the proclamation. All qualified candidates appear
on a single ballot; if no candidate receives a majority, a runoff is held.
