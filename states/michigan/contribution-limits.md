# Michigan Contribution Limits (Detailed)

> **STALENESS WARNING:** This reference was written in April 2026. Michigan contribution
> limits are adjusted periodically based on the Consumer Price Index. The figures shown
> here reflect the 2025-2026 election cycle. Verify current limits at
> https://www.michigan.gov/sos/elections/campaign-finance before making compliance decisions.

> **EDUCATIONAL DISCLAIMER:** This document is for educational and informational purposes
> only. It does not constitute legal advice. Campaigns should consult a qualified election
> law attorney or the Michigan Secretary of State's Bureau of Elections for guidance
> specific to their situation.

## Background

Michigan's campaign finance laws are codified in the Michigan Campaign Finance Act
(MCL 169.201 et seq.) and administered by the Secretary of State's Bureau of Elections.
Key features of Michigan's system:

- **Corporate and union contributions banned:** Direct treasury contributions from
  corporations and unions to candidates are prohibited. They must form PACs.
- **Separate limits by office level:** Unlike some states with a flat limit, Michigan
  has different limits for statewide, state senate, and state house candidates.
- **$20 cash cap:** One of the lowest cash contribution thresholds in the nation.
- **CPI adjustments:** Limits are adjusted periodically for inflation.

## Current Limits (2025-2026 Cycle)

All limits are **per election** (primary and general are separate elections).

### Individual Contributions to Candidates

| Office | Limit Per Election |
|--------|--------------------|
| Governor | $7,150 |
| Secretary of State | $7,150 |
| Attorney General | $7,150 |
| Lieutenant Governor | $7,150 |
| State Senator | $2,550 |
| State Representative | $1,275 |

### PAC Contributions to Candidates

| Office | Limit Per Election |
|--------|--------------------|
| Governor | $34,000 |
| Secretary of State | $34,000 |
| Attorney General | $34,000 |
| Lieutenant Governor | $34,000 |
| State Senator | $17,000 |
| State Representative | $8,500 |

PAC limits are substantially higher than individual limits in Michigan, reflecting
a deliberate policy choice in the Campaign Finance Act.

### Corporate and Union Contributions

| Source | Permitted? | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Corporate treasury | **No** | Must form a separate segregated fund (PAC) |
| Union treasury | **No** | Must form a separate segregated fund (PAC) |
| Corporate/union PAC | Yes | Subject to PAC limits above |

Michigan strictly prohibits direct corporate and union treasury contributions
to candidate committees.

### Independent Committee Contributions to Candidates

| Office | Limit Per Election |
|--------|--------------------|
| Governor | $34,000 |
| Other statewide | $34,000 |
| State Senator | $17,000 |
| State Representative | $8,500 |

Independent committees (Super PAC equivalents at the state level) are subject
to the same limits as PACs when contributing directly to candidates.

### Party Committee Contributions to Candidates

| Donor | Limit Per Election |
|-------|--------------------|
| State party (to statewide candidate) | $68,000 |
| State party (to state senator) | $34,000 |
| State party (to state rep) | $17,000 |
| County/district party | Subject to standard limits |

### Contributions to PACs and Party Committees

| Donor Type | To a PAC | To a Party Committee |
|------------|----------|---------------------|
| Individual | $41,050/year | $41,050/year |
| Corporation | **Prohibited** (direct) | **Prohibited** (direct) |
| Union | **Prohibited** (direct) | **Prohibited** (direct) |
| PAC to PAC | Subject to limits | Subject to limits |

## Cash Contribution Cap

Michigan has an exceptionally low cash contribution threshold:

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| Cash contribution limit | **$20** per contributor per election |
| Over $20 | Must use check, credit card, or other traceable method |
| Record keeping | Cash contributions must still be documented |

This $20 cap is one of the lowest in the nation and is strictly enforced.

---

## Self-Funding

Candidates may contribute **unlimited** amounts from their own personal funds to
their own campaign committee. Michigan has no self-funding trigger provision.

---

## Aggregate Limits

Michigan does **not** impose aggregate limits on the total amount a donor may
contribute across all candidates in a cycle.

---

## Special Rules

### In-Kind Contributions
In-kind contributions count against limits at fair market value.

### Loans
- Bank loans on commercially reasonable terms are not contributions
- Personal loans from individuals are contributions subject to limits
- Candidate self-loans are unlimited

### Anonymous Contributions
Contributions of $20.01 or more from anonymous/unidentified sources are prohibited.

### Bundling
Bundled contributions must be attributed to the individual donors. Each
individual donor's contribution counts against their own limit.

## Prohibited Contributions (Quick Reference)

| Source | Permitted? | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Individuals | Yes | Subject to limits by office |
| Corporations | **No** | Must use PAC |
| Unions | **No** | Must use PAC |
| PACs | Yes | Higher limits than individuals |
| Party committees | Yes | Highest limits |
| Foreign nationals | **No** | Prohibited |
| Anonymous (over $20) | **No** | Must identify donor |
| Cash (over $20) | **No** | Must use traceable method |
| State employees (soliciting subordinates) | **No** | Prohibited |
| Minors under 18 | Restricted | Limited to $20 |
