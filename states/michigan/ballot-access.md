## Overview

Michigan uses a combination of filing fees and petition signatures to regulate
ballot access. The state has an **open primary** system -- voters do not register
by party and may choose any party's ballot on primary day. Candidates must file
an Affidavit of Identity and either pay a filing fee or submit qualifying petitions.

---

## Filing Fees

Michigan offers candidates the choice of paying a filing fee OR collecting
petition signatures.

### Filing Fee Schedule

| Office | Filing Fee |
|--------|-----------|
| Governor | $100 |
| Lieutenant Governor | $100 |
| Secretary of State | $100 |
| Attorney General | $100 |
| U.S. Senator | $100 |
| U.S. Representative | $100 |
| State Senator | $100 |
| State Representative | $100 |
| County offices | Varies ($0-$100) |
| Township offices | $0 (no fee) |
| City offices | Varies by charter |
| School board | $0 (typically) |

Michigan's filing fees are notably low compared to many other states.

## Petition Alternative

Candidates who prefer not to pay the filing fee may qualify by petition:

### Petition Signature Requirements

| Office | Minimum Signatures | Maximum Signatures |
|--------|-------------------|--------------------|
| Governor | 15,000 | 30,000 |
| U.S. Senator | 15,000 | 30,000 |
| U.S. Representative | 1,000 | 2,000 |
| State Senator | 500 | 1,000 |
| State Representative | 200 | 400 |
| County offices | Varies by population | |
| Township offices | 6-100 (varies) | |

Candidates must collect at least the minimum number and may collect up to
the maximum. Collecting more than the minimum provides a buffer against
signature challenges.

### Petition Circulation Rules
- [ ] Signatures must come from registered voters in the district
- [ ] No party registration requirement (Michigan has open primaries)
- [ ] Circulator must be a registered Michigan voter
- [ ] Each petition sheet must include circulator's certificate
- [ ] Signatures valid only if collected within the 180 days before filing deadline

## Open Primary System

Michigan's open primary system has several implications for candidates:

| Feature | Details |
|---------|---------|
| Voter registration | No party registration in Michigan |
| Ballot selection | Voter chooses any one party's ballot at the primary |
| Crossover voting | Voters may choose different party ballots in different primaries |
| Candidate qualification | Candidates affiliate with a party for filing purposes |

## Affidavit of Identity

All candidates must file an Affidavit of Identity, which is a critical filing
requirement in Michigan.

### Affidavit Requirements
- [ ] Full legal name
- [ ] Residential address
- [ ] Date of birth
- [ ] Office sought and term
- [ ] Party affiliation (for partisan offices)
- [ ] Statement that candidate meets constitutional and statutory qualifications
- [ ] Notarized signature

### Affidavit Deadline
The Affidavit of Identity must be filed by the same deadline as the filing fee
payment or petition submission (typically 15 weeks before the primary).

---

## Candidate Committee Requirement

Michigan requires candidates to file a **Statement of Organization** (committee
registration) with the Bureau of Elections before collecting contributions or
making expenditures.

### Committee Formation Checklist
- [ ] Appoint a campaign treasurer
- [ ] Open a campaign bank account
- [ ] File Statement of Organization (Form DR-1) within 10 days of becoming a candidate
- [ ] A person becomes a "candidate" upon filing, raising/spending $500+, or announcing
- [ ] Must file committee registration BEFORE filing Affidavit of Identity

## Filing Deadlines (Typical Even-Year Election)

| Milestone | Timing |
|-----------|--------|
| Filing deadline (fee or petition) | 15 weeks before primary (approximately mid-April) |
| Affidavit of Identity deadline | Same as filing deadline |
| Primary election | 2nd Tuesday in August |
| General election | 1st Tuesday after 1st Monday in November |

## Write-In Candidates

| Requirement | Details |
|------------|---------|
| Declaration of intent | Must file Declaration of Intent as a Write-In Candidate |
| Filing deadline | 4:00 PM on the 2nd Friday before the election |
| Filing location | County clerk (local) or Secretary of State (state) |
| Write-in counting | Only counted if declaration filed |
| Affidavit of Identity | Not required for write-in candidates |

## Independent and Third-Party Candidates

### New/Minor Party Access

| Requirement | Details |
|------------|---------|
| Party qualification petition | Signatures equal to 1% of total gubernatorial vote |
| Alternative | Nominate by convention after qualifying |
| Filing deadline | Before the partisan primary |

### Independent Candidates (No Party)

Independent candidates qualify by petition with signature requirements similar
to major party candidates but cannot participate in the primary. They appear
directly on the general election ballot.

---

## Petition Challenge Process

| Step | Details |
|------|---------|
| Challenge deadline | Within 7 days of filing deadline |
| Filed with | Board of State Canvassers |
| Grounds | Invalid signatures, insufficient count, circulator errors |
| Review process | Bureau of Elections staff review; Board of State Canvassers decision |
| Appeal | Circuit court |

## Candidate Eligibility Checklist

- [ ] Verify age requirements for the office sought
- [ ] Confirm residency in the district (varies by office; some require 30+ days)
- [ ] Check citizenship requirements
- [ ] Review incompatible office restrictions
- [ ] File committee registration before taking any other steps
- [ ] File Affidavit of Identity by deadline
- [ ] Pay filing fee OR submit qualifying petition
