## Overview

Michigan requires candidates, PACs, party committees, independent committees, and
ballot question committees to file periodic campaign finance reports with the
Secretary of State's Bureau of Elections (BOE). The reporting schedule is built
around election dates with semi-annual filings in non-election periods.

---

## Filing Schedule

### Election Year Reports

| Report | Deadline | Covers Through |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| Pre-primary | 11th day before primary | Through 16th day before primary |
| Pre-general | 11th day before general | Through 16th day before general |
| Post-general annual | January 31 of following year | Through December 31 |

### Non-Election Year / Semi-Annual Reports

| Report | Deadline | Covers Through |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| April semi-annual | April 25 | Through April 20 |
| October semi-annual | October 25 | Through October 20 |

### Supplemental Reporting
Committees that have had no financial activity since the last report may file
a "no activity" statement instead of a full report.

---

## 48-Hour Late Contribution Reporting

Michigan requires expedited disclosure of large contributions received close
to an election:

| Trigger | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Contribution of $1,000+ received after pre-election report closing date | Report within 48 hours |
| Applies to | All candidate committees |
| Filing method | Electronic through BOE system |

This ensures late-arriving large contributions are disclosed before voters go
to the polls.

---

## Itemization Thresholds

### Contributions Received by Candidates

| Donor Type | Itemization Threshold |
|------------|----------------------|
| Individuals | $100 aggregate per election |
| PACs and committees | $50 aggregate per election |

When the threshold is exceeded, the campaign must report:
- [ ] Full name of contributor
- [ ] Complete mailing address
- [ ] Occupation and employer (for individuals)
- [ ] Date and amount of each contribution
- [ ] Aggregate total for the period

### Contributions Received by PACs

| Donor Type | Itemization Threshold |
|------------|----------------------|
| All contributors | $50 aggregate per calendar year |

### Expenditures

| Threshold | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| All expenditures over $50 | Must be itemized |
| Expenditures under $50 | May be reported in aggregate |

For each itemized expenditure:
- [ ] Payee name and address
- [ ] Date and amount
- [ ] Purpose of expenditure
- [ ] Candidate or ballot question supported/opposed (if applicable)

## Electronic Filing Through BOE

### Who Must File Electronically
- All committees that receive contributions or make expenditures of $5,000
  or more in a calendar year
- All statewide candidate committees
- All state legislative candidate committees recommended to file electronically

### Filing System
Michigan uses the **Bureau of Elections electronic filing system**. Reports are
filed online through the Secretary of State's website.

### Electronic Filing Checklist
- [ ] Register committee with Bureau of Elections (Form DR-1)
- [ ] Obtain electronic filing credentials
- [ ] Designate a treasurer (required before any fundraising or spending)
- [ ] File statement of organization within 10 days of committee formation
- [ ] Submit reports electronically by the deadline
- [ ] Retain system confirmation of each filing

---

## Who Must File

| Entity | Filing Requirement |
|--------|-------------------|
| Candidate committees | All scheduled reports |
| PACs | All scheduled reports |
| Party committees (state, county, district) | All scheduled reports |
| Independent expenditure committees | When spending exceeds $100 |
| Ballot question committees | All scheduled reports |
| Super PACs (independent expenditure only) | All scheduled reports |

### Registration Threshold
A committee must register and file reports if it receives contributions or
makes expenditures exceeding $500 in a calendar year.

---

## Late Filing Penalties

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Late filing (1-3 days) | Warning notice |
| Late filing (4+ days) | $25/day up to $1,000 |
| Failure to file | Referral to Attorney General; civil fine up to $1,000/day |
| Willful violations | Criminal penalties possible (misdemeanor) |

The Secretary of State sends late filing notices, but meeting deadlines is
the committee's responsibility.

---

## Independent Expenditure Reporting

| Requirement | Details |
|------------|---------|
| Registration | Must register committee with BOE if spending over $500 |
| Reporting threshold | Expenditures over $100 |
| Pre-election | Same schedule as candidate committees |
| Disclaimer | Must include "paid for by" identification |
| Coordination prohibition | Must be truly independent of candidate |

---

## Electioneering Communications

Michigan requires disclosure of electioneering communications that:
- Refer to a clearly identified candidate
- Are broadcast within 60 days of a general election or 30 days of a primary
- Target the candidate's electorate

---

## Public Access

Campaign finance reports are available to the public through:
- Michigan Secretary of State Campaign Finance Database (searchable online)
- In-person inspection at the Bureau of Elections
- Bulk data downloads available
- https://cfrs.michigan.gov/

---

## Record Retention

Campaigns must maintain records to verify filed reports:
- [ ] Bank statements and deposit records
- [ ] Copies of all checks received and written
- [ ] Credit card receipts and statements
- [ ] Contributor information records
- [ ] Invoices and contracts for expenditures
- [ ] Retain all records for at least 5 years after filing

---

## Common Compliance Pitfalls

- [ ] Missing the 48-hour late contribution reporting requirement
- [ ] Applying wrong itemization threshold (candidates: $100; PACs: $50)
- [ ] Failing to report in-kind contributions at fair market value
- [ ] Not registering committee within 10 days of formation
- [ ] Overlooking semi-annual reports in non-election years
