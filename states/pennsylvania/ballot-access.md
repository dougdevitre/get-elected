## Overview

Pennsylvania uses a combination of filing fees and petition signatures to regulate
ballot access. The requirements vary significantly by office level. The state has a
closed primary system (only registered party members may vote in their party's
primary), and third-party/independent access is notably more difficult than major
party access.

### Primary Petition Circulation Period
- Petitions may be circulated beginning the **10th Tuesday before the primary**
- Filing deadline: the **10th Tuesday before the primary** at 5:00 PM is the
  last day for petition circulation AND filing
- All petitions must be filed with the appropriate election office

### Primary Petition Checklist
- [ ] Obtain petition forms from county election office or Department of State
- [ ] Circulate petitions only within the designated period
- [ ] Collect signatures only from registered voters of your party in your district
- [ ] Each signature page must include circulator's affidavit
- [ ] File petition and filing fee by deadline
- [ ] File candidate affidavit and financial interest statement

## Third-Party and Independent Candidates

Third-party and independent candidates face substantially higher barriers than
major party candidates. They file nomination papers rather than primary petitions.

### Signature Requirements for Nomination Papers

| Office | Signatures Required |
|--------|---------------------|
| Governor | 2% of largest vote-getter in last statewide election |
| U.S. Senator | 2% of largest vote-getter |
| U.S. Representative | 2% of largest vote in district |
| State Senator | 2% of largest vote in district |
| State Representative | 2% of largest vote in district |

The 2% requirement can result in very high thresholds. For example, a statewide
independent candidate may need approximately 15,000-25,000 signatures depending
on turnout in the prior election.

### Nomination Paper Filing
- Filing deadline: the first Monday in August before the general election
- Nomination papers may be circulated beginning the 10th Tuesday before the primary

### Third-Party Ballot Access Challenges
Pennsylvania is known for aggressive petition challenges against third-party and
independent candidates:
- [ ] Challenges may be filed by any registered voter within 7 days of filing
- [ ] Challenges are heard by the Commonwealth Court
- [ ] Line-by-line signature review is common
- [ ] Legal costs can be assessed against the challenged candidate if the petition
      falls significantly short
- [ ] Budget for legal defense when planning a third-party run

## Candidate Eligibility Checklist

- [ ] Verify age requirements for the office sought
- [ ] Confirm residency in the district/state
- [ ] Confirm voter registration with the appropriate party (for primary)
- [ ] Review financial disclosure requirements
- [ ] Check for incompatible office restrictions
- [ ] File statement of financial interest

---

## Common Petition Pitfalls

- [ ] Signatures from voters outside the district
- [ ] Signatures from voters not registered with the candidate's party (primary)
- [ ] Missing or incomplete circulator affidavits
- [ ] Date errors on petition pages
- [ ] Insufficient total signatures (always collect 50%+ more than required)
- [ ] Filing after the deadline (strict cutoff, no extensions)
