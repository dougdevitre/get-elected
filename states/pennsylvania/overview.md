# Pennsylvania Campaign Finance Overview

> **STALENESS WARNING:** This reference reflects Pennsylvania's Election Code (Title 25) and Department of State regulations as of early 2025. Pennsylvania is notably one of the few states with no contribution limits for most races. The General Assembly periodically considers reform legislation. Always verify current requirements at [dos.pa.gov/VotingElections/](https://www.dos.pa.gov/VotingElections/).

> **EDUCATIONAL DISCLAIMER:** This is educational information, not legal advice. Pennsylvania's lack of contribution limits makes it unique among large states. Philadelphia has separate local campaign finance rules. Consult a Pennsylvania election law attorney for guidance specific to your campaign.

---

## Filing Agency

**Pennsylvania Department of State (DOS)**
- Website: [dos.pa.gov](https://www.dos.pa.gov)
- Bureau of Commissions, Elections and Legislation administers campaign finance disclosure
- Statewide and General Assembly candidates file with the DOS
- County and municipal candidates file with the **county Board of Elections**
- Philadelphia candidates are also subject to the **Philadelphia Board of Ethics**

---

## Unique Features of Pennsylvania Campaign Finance Law

1. **No contribution limits** -- Pennsylvania imposes no limits on contributions from individuals, PACs, corporations, or unions to candidates (one of only a handful of states with this policy)
2. **Disclosure-only system** -- the state relies entirely on transparency/disclosure rather than contribution caps
3. **Corporate and union contributions are permitted** -- unlike federal law and many states, corporations and unions may contribute directly to candidates without limit
4. **Philadelphia has local limits** -- the City of Philadelphia imposes contribution limits for city races, creating a dual system
5. **Electronic filing threshold** -- committees exceeding certain activity levels must file electronically; others may file on paper
6. **Broad definition of "political committee"** -- any group receiving contributions or making expenditures over $250 in a calendar year to influence elections must register

| Donor Type | To Candidate | To PAC | To Party Committee | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|--------|-------------------|-------|
| Individual | **No limit** | **No limit** | **No limit** | Disclosure required |
| Corporation | **No limit** | **No limit** | **No limit** | Direct contributions permitted |
| Labor Union | **No limit** | **No limit** | **No limit** | Direct contributions permitted |
| PAC | **No limit** | **No limit** | **No limit** | -- |
| Political Party | **No limit** | **No limit** | N/A | -- |
| Candidate to Own Campaign | **No limit** | N/A | N/A | -- |

### Philadelphia Local Contribution Limits

The City of Philadelphia imposes its own limits for **city office** races:

| Donor Type | To City Candidate (per election) | Notes |
|-----------|--------------------------------|-------|
| Individual | **$3,100** (approximate, adjusted periodically) | Per election (primary/general separate) |
| Corporation | **Prohibited** | Cannot contribute to city candidates |
| PAC | **$12,600** (approximate) | Adjusted periodically |
| Political Party (city committee) | **$12,600** (approximate) | Adjusted periodically |

*Philadelphia limits are adjusted periodically. Verify with the Philadelphia Board of Ethics at [phila.gov/departments/board-of-ethics/](https://www.phila.gov/departments/board-of-ethics/).*

## Committee Registration

### Candidate Committees
- File a **Statement of Organization** (Form DSEB-502) with the Department of State or county Board of Elections
- Must register within 20 days of receiving contributions or making expenditures exceeding $250
- Must designate a chairperson, treasurer, and depository (bank)

### Political Action Committees
- Register with the DOS when contributions received or expenditures made exceed **$250** in a calendar year
- Must file Form DSEB-502
- Must designate officers and a depository

### Party Committees
- State, county, and local party committees register with the DOS or county Board of Elections
- Subject to the same reporting requirements as other committees

## Ballot Access

### Major Party Candidates (Republican / Democrat)
- Collect **nominating petition signatures** from registered voters enrolled in the candidate's party within the district
- Signature thresholds vary by office:
  - Statewide office: **2,000 signatures**
  - State Senate: **500 signatures**
  - State House: **300 signatures**
- Petitions are circulated during a window set by statute (typically mid-February through mid-March)
- Primary elections held in May (third Tuesday in May, typically)
- Filing fee: **None** (Pennsylvania does not charge a filing fee; petitions serve as the qualifying mechanism)

### Independent and Minor Party Candidates
- Must collect **nomination papers** (distinct from nominating petitions)
- Signature thresholds are significantly higher: 2% of the largest vote cast for any statewide office in the last election in the district
- Filing deadline is typically in August
- Minor parties that polled sufficient votes in the last election may use the lower major-party petition thresholds

### Write-In Candidates
- No advance filing required; voters write in the candidate's name
- Write-in candidates who win must file a statement of candidacy after the election

## Reporting Schedule

### Cycle Year (Election Year)
| Report | Due Date | Coverage |
|--------|----------|----------|
| **Annual report** | Last Monday in January | Prior year activity |
| **1st Friday pre-primary** | 2nd Friday before primary (~10 days before) | Through prior reporting period |
| **6th Tuesday pre-primary** | 6 weeks before primary (30 days) | Through 7 weeks before primary |
| **2nd Friday pre-general** | 2nd Friday before general (~10 days before) | Through prior reporting period |
| **6th Tuesday pre-general** | 6 weeks before general (30 days) | Through 7 weeks before general |
| **Annual (year-end)** | Following January deadline | Remainder of year |

### Non-Election Year
- **Annual report** due the last Monday in January
- No interim reports required unless the committee reaches activity thresholds triggering more frequent filing

### 24-Hour Reporting
- Contributions of **$500 or more** received after the close of the last pre-election report and before the election must be reported within **24 hours** (for statewide and General Assembly races)

### Itemization Thresholds
- Contributions over **$50** from a single source must be itemized with name and address
- Contributions over **$100** require occupation and employer
- All expenditures over **$25** must be itemized

## Prohibited Contributions

Despite having no contribution limits, Pennsylvania does prohibit:
- **Cash contributions exceeding $100**
- **Anonymous contributions exceeding $25** (must be donated to the state treasury or charity)
- Contributions in the **name of another** (straw donors)
- **Foreign national** contributions (also prohibited federally)
- **Contributions made under a fictitious name**
- Philadelphia: **corporate contributions** to city candidates are prohibited
- Philadelphia: contributions exceeding the city's local limits

## Key Differences from Federal Law

| Feature | Federal | Pennsylvania |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Individual contribution limit | $3,300/election | **No limit** |
| Corporate contributions | Prohibited | **Allowed, no limit** |
| Union contributions | Prohibited (direct) | **Allowed, no limit** |
| PAC-to-candidate limit | $5,000/election | **No limit** |
| Cash cap | $100 | **$100** (same) |
| Reporting frequency | Quarterly/monthly | Annual + pre-election (30-day, 10-day) |
| Public financing | Presidential | **None** |
| Coordinated expenditure limits | Yes | **None** |
| Anonymous threshold | $50 (then itemized) | **$25** |

## Local Rules Notes

- **Philadelphia** is the major exception to Pennsylvania's no-limits regime -- the Philadelphia Board of Ethics imposes contribution limits, corporate contribution bans, and additional disclosure requirements for city races
- **Allegheny County (Pittsburgh)** follows state rules without additional local limits, though reform has been periodically discussed
- County and municipal candidates file with their **county Board of Elections**
- Some municipalities may have local ethics ordinances with campaign finance provisions -- check with the local government
- **School board** candidates are subject to the same state campaign finance rules
- **Judicial candidates** are subject to the Code of Judicial Conduct in addition to campaign finance law

---

## Electronic Filing

- Electronic filing through the DOS Campaign Finance Online Filing System is **required** for committees that receive contributions or make expenditures of **$250 or more** in a reporting period (the threshold has been this low, effectively requiring electronic filing for most active committees)
- Smaller committees may file on paper
- The electronic filing system is available at the DOS website
- Philadelphia candidates must also file with the Philadelphia Board of Ethics' electronic system
