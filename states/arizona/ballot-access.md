# Arizona Ballot Access (Detailed)

> **STALENESS WARNING:** This reference was written in April 2026. Arizona ballot access
> requirements, signature thresholds, and deadlines are subject to change. Verify current
> requirements at https://azsos.gov/elections before filing.

> **EDUCATIONAL DISCLAIMER:** This document is for educational and informational purposes
> only. It does not constitute legal advice. Candidates should consult a qualified election
> law attorney or the Arizona Secretary of State's office for guidance specific to their
> situation.

## Overview

Arizona uses nomination petitions as the primary ballot access mechanism. The number
of required signatures varies by office, party, and district size. Candidates running
through the Clean Elections system must also meet qualifying contribution requirements.
Arizona has a semi-closed primary system where registered party members vote in their
party's primary, and independents may choose a party ballot.

---

## Nomination Petition Signature Requirements

### Major Party Candidates (Republican/Democratic)

Signature requirements are calculated as a percentage of registered party voters
in the relevant jurisdiction. The Secretary of State publishes exact numbers
before each cycle.

| Office | Approximate Signatures Required |
|--------|-------------------------------|
| Governor | 5,610-7,945 (varies by party registration) |
| U.S. Senator | 5,610-7,945 |
| Secretary of State | 3,587-4,882 |
| Attorney General | 3,587-4,882 |
| Other statewide offices | 3,587-4,882 |
| U.S. Representative | Varies by district (typically 668-1,403) |
| State Senator | Varies by district (typically 290-520) |
| State Representative | Varies by district (typically 290-520) |

### Signature Calculation Formula

| Factor | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Base | Registered voters of the candidate's party in the district |
| Governor/U.S. Senator | 0.25% to 0.5% of party registration statewide |
| Other statewide | 0.125% to 0.25% of party registration statewide |
| Legislative | 0.5% to 1.0% of party registration in district |

The Secretary of State publishes minimum and maximum signature ranges before
each election. Candidates must collect at least the minimum.

## Filing Fees

Arizona charges a nominal filing fee in addition to petition signatures:

| Office | Filing Fee |
|--------|-----------|
| Governor | $500 |
| U.S. Senator | $500 |
| Other statewide | $100 |
| U.S. Representative | $300 |
| State Senator | $100 |
| State Representative | $100 |

## Petition Circulation and Filing Deadlines

| Milestone | Timing |
|-----------|--------|
| Petitions available | Varies (check SOS website) |
| Earliest circulation start | Typically early in the election year |
| Filing deadline | 120 days before the primary (approximately early April) |
| Primary election | Late July/early August |

### Petition Checklist
- [ ] Obtain official petition forms from the Secretary of State
- [ ] Verify signature range for your specific office and party
- [ ] Circulate petitions only among registered voters of your party in your district
- [ ] Each petition sheet must include circulator's affidavit
- [ ] Circulator must be a qualified elector of Arizona
- [ ] Collect 20-30% more signatures than the minimum to survive challenges
- [ ] File petitions with Secretary of State by the deadline
- [ ] Retain copies of all filed petition sheets

## Clean Elections Qualifying Contributions

Clean Elections candidates must collect a designated number of $5 qualifying
contributions instead of (or in addition to) standard petitions.

| Office | $5 Qualifying Contributions Required |
|--------|--------------------------------------|
| Governor | ~4,000 |
| Other statewide offices | ~1,500 |
| State Senator | ~250 |
| State Representative | ~200 |

### Clean Elections Qualifying Checklist
- [ ] Declare intent to run as Clean Elections candidate with CCEC
- [ ] File nomination petition (reduced requirement may apply)
- [ ] Collect $5 qualifying contributions from registered voters in district
- [ ] Each contribution must be documented on official CCEC form
- [ ] Submit qualifying contributions to CCEC for verification
- [ ] Meet the qualifying deadline (before petition filing deadline)
- [ ] Receive certification from CCEC before receiving public funds

## Independent and Minor Party Candidates

### Independent Candidates

| Office | Signatures Required |
|--------|---------------------|
| Governor | ~36,000 (3% of registered independents statewide) |
| U.S. Senator | ~36,000 |
| U.S. Representative | Varies by district |
| State legislative | Varies by district |

Independent candidates face significantly higher petition requirements than
major party candidates.

### Recognized Minor Parties
Minor parties that have maintained ballot status may nominate candidates under
the same rules as major parties, with signature requirements based on their
party's registration numbers (often much lower).

---

## Write-In Candidates

| Requirement | Details |
|------------|---------|
| Declaration of write-in candidacy | Must file with Secretary of State or county |
| Filing deadline | 40 days before the election (general); 76 days before (primary) |
| Filing fee | Same as petition candidates |
| Write-in counting | Only counted if declaration filed |

---

## Petition Challenge Process

| Step | Details |
|------|---------|
| Challenge deadline | 10 business days after filing deadline |
| Filed with | Superior Court in the county of the candidate's residence |
| Grounds | Invalid signatures, insufficient count, circulator errors |
| Timeline | Court must rule within 5 business days |
| Cost | Challenger bears own costs unless specified |
