# Arizona Contribution Limits (Detailed)

> **STALENESS WARNING:** This reference was written in April 2026. Arizona contribution
> limits are adjusted biennially. The figures shown here reflect the 2025-2026 election
> cycle. Verify current limits at https://azsos.gov/elections and https://www.azcleanelections.gov
> before making compliance decisions.

> **EDUCATIONAL DISCLAIMER:** This document is for educational and informational purposes
> only. It does not constitute legal advice. Campaigns should consult a qualified election
> law attorney, the Arizona Secretary of State, or the Citizens Clean Elections Commission
> for guidance specific to their situation.

---

## Background

Arizona has a dual campaign finance system:

- **Traditional (privately funded) candidates** operate under contribution limits
  set by the Secretary of State and adjusted biennially.
- **Clean Elections (publicly funded) candidates** receive public funding through
  the Citizens Clean Elections Commission (CCEC) after qualifying with small-dollar
  contributions. Clean Elections candidates may not accept private contributions
  beyond the qualifying phase.

This document covers both systems.

---

## Traditional (Privately Funded) Candidate Limits

All limits are **per election cycle** (primary and general combined).

### Individual Contributions to Candidates

| Office | Limit Per Election Cycle |
|--------|--------------------------|
| Governor | $6,390 |
| Other statewide offices | $6,390 |
| State Senator | $5,300 |
| State Representative | $5,300 |
| Legislative candidates (general) | Limits above cover full cycle |

### Corporate Contributions

| Source | Permitted? | Limit |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Corporate treasury | **Yes** | Same as individual limits |
| Union treasury | **Yes** | Same as individual limits |
| LLC contributions | **Yes** | Attributed to owners; same limits apply |

Arizona allows direct corporate and union treasury contributions to candidates.

### PAC Contributions to Candidates

| Office | Limit Per Election Cycle |
|--------|--------------------------|
| Statewide offices | $6,390 |
| State legislative offices | $5,300 |

### Mega PAC Doubled Limits

Arizona has a provision that doubles contribution limits for qualifying "Mega PACs":

| Requirement | Details |
|------------|---------|
| Qualification | PAC must have received contributions from 500+ individuals in prior cycle |
| Effect | May contribute double the standard PAC limit |
| Statewide doubled limit | ~$12,780 |
| Legislative doubled limit | ~$10,600 |

### Party Committee Contributions

| Donor | Limit |
|-------|-------|
| State party committee | **No limit** to candidates |
| County party committee | Subject to standard limits |
| Legislative district committee | Subject to standard limits |

## Clean Elections Public Financing

### How Clean Elections Works

| Step | Details |
|------|---------|
| 1. Declaration | Candidate declares intent to run as Clean Elections candidate |
| 2. Qualifying contributions | Collect $5 qualifying contributions from registered voters in district |
| 3. Qualification threshold | Meet required number of $5 contributions |
| 4. Public funding | Receive public funding for primary and general elections |
| 5. Spending cap | Must limit spending to the public funding amount |

### Qualifying Contribution Requirements

| Office | Number of $5 Qualifying Contributions |
|--------|--------------------------------------|
| Governor | ~4,000 |
| Other statewide offices | ~1,500 |
| State Senator | ~250 |
| State Representative | ~200 |

### Clean Elections Candidate Restrictions
- [ ] May not accept private contributions beyond early seed money (limited)
- [ ] Must participate in debates organized by CCEC
- [ ] Must limit spending to the public funding amount
- [ ] Must return any unspent funds after the election
- [ ] Subject to CCEC auditing and compliance requirements

### Early Seed Money (Clean Elections)

| Office | Maximum Seed Money | Individual Contribution Cap |
|--------|-------------------|---------------------------|
| Governor | Varies | $200 per contributor |
| Statewide offices | Varies | $200 per contributor |
| Legislative offices | Varies | $200 per contributor |

Seed money may be collected before qualifying to fund the qualifying effort.

---

## Self-Funding

Traditional candidates may contribute unlimited amounts from personal funds.
Clean Elections candidates may NOT self-fund beyond the seed money limits.

---

## Aggregate Limits

Arizona does **not** impose aggregate limits on the total amount a donor may
give across all candidates.

---

## Special Rules

### In-Kind Contributions
Count against limits at fair market value.

### Loans
- Bank loans on commercial terms are not contributions
- Personal loans are contributions subject to limits
- Candidate self-loans are unlimited (traditional candidates)

### Cash Contributions
Cash contributions over $100 are prohibited.

## Prohibited Contributions (Quick Reference)

| Source | Permitted? | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Individuals | Yes | Subject to limits |
| Corporations | Yes | Subject to limits |
| Unions | Yes | Subject to limits |
| PACs | Yes | Subject to limits; Mega PAC doubled |
| State party committees | Yes | **No limit** |
| Foreign nationals | **No** | Prohibited |
| Anonymous (over $50) | **No** | Must identify donor |
| Cash (over $100) | **No** | Must use traceable method |
| Public utilities | Restricted | Additional rules apply |
