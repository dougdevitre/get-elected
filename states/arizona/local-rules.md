# Arizona Local Election Rules (Detailed)

> **STALENESS WARNING:** This reference was written in April 2026. Arizona local election
> rules vary by municipality and county. Charter cities may have rules that differ from
> state defaults. Verify current local requirements with the appropriate county recorder
> or city clerk before filing.

> **EDUCATIONAL DISCLAIMER:** This document is for educational and informational purposes
> only. It does not constitute legal advice. Candidates should consult a qualified election
> law attorney or the relevant local election authority for guidance specific to their
> situation.

## Overview

Arizona has 15 counties and over 90 incorporated cities and towns. Local elections
are generally **nonpartisan** at the municipal level, while county offices are
partisan. Charter cities may adopt their own election rules within the framework
of state law. Arizona's Clean Elections system applies only to state-level offices,
not to local races.

## City of Phoenix

### Government Structure

| Office | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Mayor | Nonpartisan, 4-year term |
| City Council | 8 districts, nonpartisan, 4-year terms (staggered) |
| Election type | Nonpartisan; runoff if no majority in primary |
| Election timing | Odd-numbered years (March primary, May runoff) |

### Phoenix Election Rules
- [ ] Nonpartisan elections -- no party affiliation on ballot
- [ ] March primary; if no candidate receives majority, top two advance to May runoff
- [ ] Campaign contribution limits set by city charter and ordinance
- [ ] Campaign finance reports filed with Phoenix City Clerk
- [ ] Phoenix public campaign financing program (limited matching funds)

### Phoenix Contribution Limits

| Donor Type | Limit |
|------------|-------|
| Individual to candidate | Varies (set by city; check current Phoenix ordinance) |
| PAC to candidate | Same as individual |
| Corporate | Subject to city rules |
| Self-funding | No limit |

## City of Tucson

### Government Structure

| Office | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Mayor | Nonpartisan, 4-year term |
| City Council | 6 wards, nonpartisan, 4-year terms (staggered) |
| Election type | Ward-based primary, city-wide general |
| Election timing | Odd-numbered years |

### Tucson Election Rules
- [ ] Ward primary elections -- only ward residents vote in primary
- [ ] General election -- all city voters vote on each ward race
- [ ] Nonpartisan ballot
- [ ] Campaign finance reports filed with Tucson City Clerk
- [ ] State contribution limits apply unless city has stricter rules

### Tucson-Specific Notes
- Tucson is a charter city with its own election code
- The city has debated and occasionally adopted local public financing provisions
- City races do not participate in the state Clean Elections program

### Elected County Offices

| Office | Details |
|--------|---------|

| Sheriff | Partisan, 4-year term |

## Charter City Variations

Arizona distinguishes between charter cities and general-law cities:

| Feature | General-Law City | Charter City |
|---------|-----------------|--------------|
| Election rules | State default | May customize |
| Contribution limits | State limits apply | May set own (stricter or same) |
| Term limits | State default | May set own |
| Election timing | Per state schedule | May set own |
| Recall provisions | State default | May customize |
| Council structure | As specified by state | Flexible |

### Notable Charter Cities
- Phoenix, Tucson, Mesa, Tempe, Scottsdale, Chandler, Glendale, Flagstaff

## Nonpartisan Municipal Elections

Most Arizona cities and towns hold nonpartisan elections:

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| Party affiliation | Not listed on ballot |
| Primary | All candidates run together; majority wins or top two advance |
| General/runoff | Top two candidates if no majority in primary |
| Timing | Varies by city (commonly odd-numbered years) |

## School Board and Special District Elections

### School Boards

| Detail | Rule |
|--------|------|
| Election type | Nonpartisan |
| Term length | 4 years |
| Board size | 3 or 5 members (governing boards) |
| Filing | Nomination petitions filed with county school superintendent |
| Campaign finance | State rules apply |

### Special Districts

| District Type | Election Details |
|--------------|-----------------|
| Fire districts | Nonpartisan, 4-year terms |
| Water/irrigation districts | Per district-specific rules |
| Community college districts | Nonpartisan, varies |
| Hospital districts | Nonpartisan |

### Special District Checklist
- [ ] Determine specific district election rules (varies significantly)
- [ ] File nomination petition with appropriate authority
- [ ] Register campaign committee if raising/spending funds
- [ ] State disclosure rules generally apply
- [ ] Check for district-specific contribution rules
