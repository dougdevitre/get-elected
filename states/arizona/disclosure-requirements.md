# Arizona Disclosure Requirements (Detailed)

> **STALENESS WARNING:** This reference was written in April 2026. Arizona disclosure
> requirements are governed by both the Secretary of State and the Citizens Clean Elections
> Commission. Proposition 211 (2022) added new dark money disclosure rules. Verify
> current requirements at https://azsos.gov/elections and https://www.azcleanelections.gov
> before making compliance decisions.

> **EDUCATIONAL DISCLAIMER:** This document is for educational and informational purposes
> only. It does not constitute legal advice. Campaigns should consult a qualified election
> law attorney or the appropriate state agency for guidance specific to their situation.

## Overview

Arizona has a layered disclosure system reflecting its dual traditional/Clean Elections
framework and the addition of Proposition 211's dark money transparency requirements.
Traditional candidates file with the Secretary of State, while Clean Elections
candidates have separate reporting through the Citizens Clean Elections Commission (CCEC).

---

## Traditional Candidate Reporting Schedule

### Quarterly Reports

| Report | Deadline | Covers Through |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| Q1 (January-March) | April 15 | March 31 |
| Q2 (April-June) | July 15 | June 30 |
| Q3 (July-September) | October 15 | September 30 |
| Q4 (October-December) | January 31 | December 31 |

### Pre-Election Reports

| Report | Deadline | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Pre-primary | 10 days before primary | Covers through 17 days before primary |
| Pre-general | 10 days before general | Covers through 17 days before general |

### Post-Election Report
- Due within 60 days of the general election
- Covers period from last pre-election report through 45 days after election

---

## Clean Elections Candidate Reporting

Clean Elections candidates file separate reports through the CCEC system.

### CCEC Reporting Schedule

| Report | Timing |
|--------|--------|
| Qualifying period report | Throughout qualifying phase |
| Pre-primary | 10 days before primary |
| Pre-general | 10 days before general |
| Post-election | 60 days after general |

### CCEC-Specific Requirements
- [ ] All qualifying contributions ($5 each) must be individually reported
- [ ] Expenditures tracked against public funding amount
- [ ] Real-time reporting during qualifying period
- [ ] Surplus funds must be returned after election
- [ ] Subject to CCEC audit

## Itemization Thresholds

### Traditional Candidates

| Type | Itemization Threshold |
|------|----------------------|
| Contributions from individuals | $500 aggregate per cycle |
| Contributions from entities | $500 aggregate per cycle |
| Expenditures | $250 aggregate per cycle |

### Clean Elections Candidates

| Type | Itemization Threshold |
|------|----------------------|
| Qualifying contributions ($5) | All itemized (every $5 contribution) |
| Seed money contributions | $100 aggregate per cycle |
| Expenditures | All itemized (public funds accountability) |

When the threshold is exceeded, report:
- [ ] Full name of contributor
- [ ] Complete mailing address
- [ ] Occupation and employer (individuals)
- [ ] Date and amount of each contribution
- [ ] Aggregate cycle total

## Proposition 211: Dark Money Disclosure

Arizona voters approved Proposition 211 in November 2022, creating significant
new disclosure requirements for "dark money" spending.

### Key Provisions

| Requirement | Details |
|------------|---------|
| Threshold | Applies to organizations spending $50,000+ on statewide campaigns or $25,000+ on legislative campaigns |
| Disclosure | Must identify original source donors of $5,000+ |
| "Follow the money" | Requires tracing funds back through intermediary organizations |
| Enforcement | CCEC has enforcement authority |
| Penalties | Treble damages (3x the undisclosed amount) |

### Prop 211 Checklist
- [ ] Determine if spending meets threshold for statewide or legislative campaigns
- [ ] Identify all donors of $5,000+ to the organization
- [ ] Trace funds through intermediary organizations if applicable
- [ ] File disclosure with CCEC
- [ ] Maintain records of original source donors

## Electronic Filing

### Filing Systems

| Candidate Type | System |
|---------------|--------|
| Traditional candidates | Secretary of State e-filing system |
| Clean Elections candidates | CCEC reporting system |
| Independent expenditure groups | Secretary of State system |
| Prop 211 disclosures | CCEC system |

### Electronic Filing Checklist
- [ ] Register committee with Secretary of State
- [ ] Obtain e-filing system credentials
- [ ] Designate treasurer
- [ ] File statement of organization
- [ ] Submit reports by deadline (11:59 PM)
- [ ] Retain confirmation of electronic submission

---

## Independent Expenditure Reporting

| Threshold | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| $1,000+ in aggregate | Must file independent expenditure report |
| Within 16 days of election | File within 24 hours |
| Disclaimer | Must include "paid for by" identification on communications |

---

## Late Filing Penalties

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Late filing | $10/day for first 15 days |
| Late filing (16+ days) | $25/day |
| Willful failure to file | Class 1 misdemeanor |
| Clean Elections violations | CCEC enforcement; potential repayment of public funds |
