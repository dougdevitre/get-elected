# Georgia Disclosure Requirements (Detailed)

> **STALENESS WARNING:** This reference was written in April 2026. Georgia disclosure
> schedules and thresholds may change through legislative action. Verify current
> requirements at https://ethics.ga.gov before making compliance decisions.

> **EDUCATIONAL DISCLAIMER:** This document is for educational and informational purposes
> only. It does not constitute legal advice. Campaigns should consult a qualified election
> law attorney or the Georgia Government Transparency and Campaign Finance Commission
> for guidance specific to their situation.

## Overview

Georgia requires all candidates, campaign committees, PACs, and independent committees
to file periodic campaign finance disclosure reports with the Georgia Government
Transparency and Campaign Finance Commission. The reporting schedule is structured
around election dates with semi-annual filings in non-election periods.

---

## Filing Schedule

### Semi-Annual Reports (All Committees)

| Report | Deadline | Covers Through |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| June semi-annual | June 30 | Through May 31 |
| December semi-annual | December 31 | Through November 30 |

### Pre-Election Reports

| Report | Deadline | Covers Through |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| Pre-primary | 15 days before primary | Through 20 days before primary |
| Pre-general | 15 days before general | Through 20 days before general |
| Pre-runoff | 15 days before runoff | Through 20 days before runoff |

### Supplemental Pre-Election Reports

| Report | Deadline | Covers Through |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| Supplemental pre-primary | 6 days before primary | Through 8 days before primary |
| Supplemental pre-general | 6 days before general | Through 8 days before general |
| Supplemental pre-runoff | 6 days before runoff | Through 8 days before runoff |

The supplemental reports capture late-arriving contributions and expenditures
between the regular pre-election report and the election itself.

---

## 24-Hour Large Contribution Reporting

Georgia requires rapid disclosure of large contributions received close to an election:

| Trigger | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Contribution of $1,000+ received within 15 days of election | Report within 24 hours |
| Applies to | All candidate committees |
| Filing method | Electronic through GEFS |

This ensures voters have timely information about major donations arriving
in the final days before an election.

---

## Itemization Thresholds

### Contributions Received

| Type | Itemization Threshold |
|------|----------------------|
| All contributions | $100 aggregate per election cycle |

When a contributor exceeds $100 in aggregate, the campaign must report:
- [ ] Full name of contributor
- [ ] Mailing address
- [ ] Occupation and employer (for individuals)
- [ ] Date and amount of each contribution
- [ ] Aggregate year-to-date total

### Expenditures

| Threshold | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Expenditures over $100 | Must be itemized |
| Expenditures under $100 | May be reported in aggregate |

For each itemized expenditure:
- [ ] Payee name and address
- [ ] Date and amount
- [ ] Purpose of expenditure

---

## Electronic Filing Through GEFS

Georgia uses the **Georgia Electronic Filing System (GEFS)** for campaign finance
reporting.

### GEFS Requirements
- [ ] All state-level candidates and committees must file electronically
- [ ] Local candidates may file on paper or electronically (varies by jurisdiction)
- [ ] Access GEFS at https://ethics.ga.gov
- [ ] Treasurer must register for system access
- [ ] Reports filed by 11:59 PM on the due date

### Registration Checklist
- [ ] File Campaign Committee Registration (CCR-1) within 15 days of committee formation
- [ ] Designate campaign treasurer
- [ ] Designate campaign depository (bank account)
- [ ] Obtain GEFS login credentials
- [ ] Confirm committee type (candidate, PAC, independent, party)

---

## Who Must File

| Entity | Filing Authority |
|--------|-----------------|
| Statewide candidates | Georgia Campaign Finance Commission |
| State legislative candidates | Georgia Campaign Finance Commission |
| County/local candidates | County election superintendent (or Commission) |
| PACs (state-level) | Georgia Campaign Finance Commission |
| Party committees | Georgia Campaign Finance Commission |
| Independent expenditure committees | Georgia Campaign Finance Commission |

---

## Late Filing Penalties

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Late filing (per day) | $50/day or 1% of total receipts/expenditures, whichever is greater |
| Maximum penalty | $10,000 per report |
| Failure to file | Additional penalties; potential criminal prosecution |

The Commission may waive or reduce penalties for good cause shown.

---

## Independent Expenditure Reporting

| Requirement | Details |
|------------|---------|
| Registration | Must register with Commission if spending over $500 |
| Reporting threshold | Expenditures over $500 in aggregate |
| Pre-election reports | Same schedule as candidate committees |
| Disclaimer required | "Paid for by [organization name]" |

---

## Lobbyist Disclosure (Related)

Lobbyists must file separate disclosure reports with the Commission:
- Annual registration
- Periodic expenditure reports
- Session-period contribution restrictions (see contribution-limits.md)

---

## Public Access

Campaign finance reports are publicly available through:
- GEFS online search (searchable by candidate, committee, or contributor)
- In-person inspection at the Commission offices
- Bulk data downloads available

---

## Record Retention

- [ ] Maintain all financial records for at least 5 years
- [ ] Keep bank statements, deposit slips, and canceled checks
- [ ] Retain copies of all contribution checks or electronic records
- [ ] Keep invoices and receipts for all expenditures
- [ ] Preserve contributor information cards or database records
