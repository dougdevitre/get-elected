# New York Campaign Finance Overview

> **STALENESS WARNING:** This reference reflects New York Election Law and Board of Elections rules as of early 2025. New York enacted significant campaign finance reforms in 2019-2024, including a new public financing program for statewide races beginning in 2026. Contribution limits are adjusted periodically. NYC Campaign Finance Board rules are separate from state rules and change independently. Always verify current requirements at [elections.ny.gov](https://www.elections.ny.gov) and [nyccfb.info](https://www.nyccfb.info).

> **EDUCATIONAL DISCLAIMER:** This is educational information, not legal advice. New York has a complex dual system of state and NYC campaign finance regulation. The NYC public matching funds program has detailed compliance requirements. Consult a New York election law attorney for guidance specific to your campaign.

---

## Filing Agencies

### State Level
**New York State Board of Elections (NYSBOE)**
- Website: [elections.ny.gov](https://www.elections.ny.gov)
- Administers campaign finance disclosure for all state and local candidates outside NYC (and state-level candidates in NYC)
- Operates the Campaign Finance Filing System

### New York City
**New York City Campaign Finance Board (NYC CFB)**
- Website: [nyccfb.info](https://www.nyccfb.info)
- Administers the city's public matching funds program
- NYC candidates for city office file with the CFB if participating in the public matching program, and also file with NYSBOE
- Provides extensive compliance training and support

---

## Unique Features of New York Campaign Finance Law

1. **Dual filing system** -- NYC candidates file with both the state BOE and the NYC CFB
2. **NYC 8:1 public matching funds** -- NYC provides an 8-to-1 match on small-dollar contributions from NYC residents for participating candidates (up to certain limits per contributor)
3. **Statewide public financing launching in 2026** -- New York's new statewide public financing program takes effect for the 2026 election cycle
4. **LLCs treated as corporations** -- since 2019, LLCs are treated as corporations for contribution purposes (previously treated as individuals, which was a major loophole)
5. **Housekeeping accounts** -- state party committees can maintain "housekeeping" accounts for non-campaign administrative expenses, but these have been subject to reform
6. **"Pay-to-play" restrictions** -- government contractors face contribution restrictions in NYC

## NYC 8:1 Public Matching Funds Program

### How It Works
- The NYC CFB matches the first **$175** of each contribution from a NYC resident at an **8:1 ratio** for City Council races (thresholds vary by office)
- A $175 contribution generates $1,400 in matching funds, for a total of $1,575 to the campaign
- Maximum matchable amount varies by office (e.g., first $250 for citywide offices)
- Only contributions from **NYC residents** are matchable
- Contributions from PACs, corporations, LLCs, and partnerships are **not matchable**

### Eligibility Requirements
- Candidate must agree to **spending limits** set by the CFB
- Must meet a **fundraising threshold** (minimum number of contributors and total raised) to receive matching funds
- Must participate in **CFB-sponsored debates**
- Must comply with detailed **record-keeping and reporting** requirements
- Must return unspent funds after the election

### Spending Limits (Participating Candidates, Approximate)
| Office | Spending Limit per Election |
|--------|---------------------------|
| Mayor | ~$7.7 million (primary), ~$7.7 million (general) |
| Comptroller / Public Advocate | ~$4.9 million |
| Borough President | ~$1.6 million |
| City Council | ~$264,000 |

*Limits are adjusted by the CFB and may change each cycle. Verify at nyccfb.info.*

## Statewide Public Financing (2026 Launch)

New York's new **Public Campaign Finance Board** will administer a statewide matching funds program starting in 2026:

- Applies to candidates for Governor, Lt. Governor, Attorney General, Comptroller, State Senate, and State Assembly
- Matching ratios and thresholds are set by the Public Campaign Finance Board
- Participating candidates must agree to lower contribution limits and spending caps
- The program is modeled on the NYC CFB system but with state-specific parameters
- Regulations and implementation details are still being finalized as of early 2025

## Committee Registration

### State Level
- File a **Filer Registration** (Form CF-02) with NYSBOE
- Must register before receiving contributions or making expenditures
- Must designate a treasurer
- Authorized committees, PACs, party committees, and independent expenditure committees all register through NYSBOE

### NYC Level (Participating Candidates)
- File a **Certification** with the NYC CFB to participate in the matching funds program
- Certification deadline is typically well before the election (check CFB calendar)
- Must also register with NYSBOE separately
- CFB assigns a liaison to each participating campaign for compliance guidance

## Ballot Access

### Major Party Candidates (Democrat / Republican)
- Collect **designating petition signatures** from enrolled party members in the district
- Signature requirements vary by office (e.g., 5% of enrolled party voters or 15,000, whichever is less, for statewide; lower thresholds for legislative seats)
- Petitions are circulated during a specific window (typically June) set by the BOE
- Primary elections are held in late June (for state/local races; federal primaries may differ)

### Independent and Minor Party Candidates
- Must collect **independent nominating petition signatures** from registered voters in the district
- Threshold: varies by office, typically higher than designating petitions
- Petitions circulated during a separate window
- Opportunity-to-ballot petitions for new party status require meeting vote thresholds in gubernatorial elections

### Write-In Candidates
- Must file a certificate of acceptance after the election if they win
- No advance filing required in most cases, but some local jurisdictions have rules

## Reporting Schedule

### State Level (NYSBOE)
- **32-day pre-primary** report
- **11-day pre-primary** report
- **32-day pre-general** report
- **11-day pre-general** report
- **27-day post-general** report
- **January periodic** report (covers previous July-January)
- **July periodic** report (covers previous January-July)

### NYC CFB Reporting
- NYC participating candidates file with the CFB on a schedule set by the CFB, typically more frequent than state filings
- Disclosure statements are due multiple times per election cycle
- The CFB requires **daily transaction entry** into its electronic filing system (C-SMART) during the final weeks before an election

### Itemization
- Contributions over **$99** from a single source must be itemized
- Contributor name, address, occupation, and employer required for itemized contributions
- All expenditures over **$50** must be itemized

---

## Prohibited Contributions

- **Corporate contributions exceeding $5,000/year** (aggregate to all candidates and committees)
- **LLC contributions exceeding corporate limits** (LLCs treated as corporations since 2019)
- Contributions in the **name of another** (straw donors)
- **Foreign national** contributions
- **Cash contributions exceeding $100**
- For NYC matching funds participants: **corporate, LLC, and partnership contributions** are prohibited entirely
- Contributions from entities **doing business with the City** face reduced limits for NYC matching participants (the "doing business" database limits contributions to a fraction of standard limits)

## Key Differences from Federal Law

| Feature | Federal | New York State | NYC (Matching Participants) |
|---------|---------|---------------|---------------------------|
| Individual contribution limit | $3,300/election | $9,400-$69,700/election (varies by office) | $1,000-$2,000/election |
| Corporate contributions | Prohibited | **Allowed** ($5,000/year cap) | **Prohibited** |
| LLC treatment | Treated as partnerships/individuals | **Treated as corporations** | **Prohibited** |
| Public financing | Presidential only | **Launching 2026** (statewide) | **8:1 matching** |
| Cash cap | $100 | **$100** | **$100** |
| Reporting | Quarterly/monthly | 32-day/11-day pre-election + periodic | CFB schedule (more frequent) |

## Local Rules Notes

- **NYC** has the most comprehensive local campaign finance system in the nation -- the CFB system is effectively a separate regulatory regime layered on top of state law
- **Nassau, Suffolk, Westchester** and other suburban counties follow state BOE rules without additional local overlay (though some municipalities may have local provisions)
- **Doing business with NYC** triggers reduced contribution limits -- check the NYC CFB "Doing Business" database
- Local party committees have specific rules about housekeeping accounts and transfers
- Judicial candidates follow a separate set of rules under the NYS Administrative Board of the Courts
