## Overview

Ohio requires candidates, PACs, party committees, and other political entities to
file periodic campaign finance reports with the Secretary of State or the appropriate
county board of elections. The reporting schedule is built around election dates with
additional semi-annual filings.

---

## Filing Schedule

### Pre-Election Reports

| Report | Deadline | Covers Through |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| Pre-primary | 12 days before primary | Through 20 days before primary |
| Pre-general | 12 days before general | Through 20 days before general |

### Post-Election Reports

| Report | Deadline | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Post-general | January 31 of following year | Covers through December 31 |

### Semi-Annual Reports

| Report | Deadline | Covers Through |
|--------|----------|----------------|
| Mid-year (non-election year) | July 31 | Through June 30 |
| Year-end | January 31 | Through December 31 |

### Special Election Reports
When a special election is called, the Secretary of State issues a specific
reporting schedule. Generally follows the same 12-day pre-election pattern.

## Itemization Thresholds

### Contributions Received

| Donor Type | Itemization Threshold |
|------------|----------------------|
| Individuals | $100 per year aggregate |
| Political entities (PACs, parties) | $25 per year aggregate |

When a contributor's aggregate contributions exceed the threshold, the campaign
must report:
- [ ] Full name of contributor
- [ ] Complete mailing address
- [ ] Employer and occupation (for individuals)
- [ ] Date and amount of each contribution
- [ ] Aggregate total for the reporting period

### Expenditures

| Threshold | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| All expenditures over $25 | Must be itemized |
| Expenditures under $25 | May be reported in aggregate |

For each itemized expenditure, the report must include:
- [ ] Payee name and address
- [ ] Date and amount
- [ ] Purpose of expenditure

## Electronic Filing

### Who Must File Electronically
- All statewide candidates
- All state legislative candidates
- All state PACs and party committees
- County and local candidates filing with the SOS

### Filing System
Ohio uses the **Secretary of State Online Filing System** for electronic reports.
The system is accessible at https://www.ohiosos.gov/campaign-finance/.

### Filing Checklist
- [ ] Register committee with Secretary of State
- [ ] Obtain login credentials for online filing system
- [ ] Designate a treasurer (required before any fundraising)
- [ ] File statement of organization within 10 days of committee formation
- [ ] Submit reports by 11:59 PM on the due date
- [ ] Retain records for at least 6 years

---

## Late Filing Penalties

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Late filing (1-7 days) | $50 per day |
| Late filing (8-14 days) | $100 per day |
| Late filing (15+ days) | Referred to Ohio Elections Commission |
| Failure to file | Civil penalties up to $10,000; potential criminal referral |

The Ohio Elections Commission (OEC) has enforcement authority over campaign
finance violations. Complaints may be filed by any person.

---

## Independent Expenditure Reporting

Organizations making independent expenditures (not coordinated with a candidate)
must file reports when spending exceeds certain thresholds:

| Threshold | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Over $1,000 in aggregate | File independent expenditure report |
| Within 20 days of election | File within 24 hours of expenditure |

---

## Electioneering Communications

Ohio requires disclosure of electioneering communications that:
- Refer to a clearly identified candidate
- Are made within 30 days of a primary or 60 days of a general election
- Are targeted to the relevant electorate

---

## Public Access to Reports

All campaign finance reports are available to the public through:
- Ohio SOS online database (searchable)
- In-person inspection at county boards of elections
- Bulk data downloads available for researchers

---

## Record Retention Checklist

Campaigns must maintain records sufficient to verify reports:
- [ ] Bank statements and deposit slips
- [ ] Copies of all checks received and written
- [ ] Credit card receipts and statements
- [ ] Invoices and contracts for expenditures
- [ ] Contributor information cards or records
- [ ] Retain all records for at least 6 years after filing
