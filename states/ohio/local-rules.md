# Ohio Local Election Rules (Detailed)

> **STALENESS WARNING:** This reference was written in April 2026. Ohio local election
> rules vary significantly by municipality and county. Charter cities may have rules that
> differ from state defaults. Verify current local requirements with the appropriate
> county board of elections before filing.

> **EDUCATIONAL DISCLAIMER:** This document is for educational and informational purposes
> only. It does not constitute legal advice. Candidates should consult a qualified election
> law attorney or the relevant county board of elections for guidance specific to their
> situation.

## Overview

Ohio has 88 counties, hundreds of municipalities, and thousands of townships and
special districts. Local election rules are governed by a combination of state law
(Ohio Revised Code) and local charters. Ohio's "home rule" provision allows
municipalities to adopt charters that modify default election procedures.

## Cuyahoga County (Cleveland)

### County Government
Cuyahoga County operates under a charter government (adopted 2009) with an elected
county executive and 11-member county council.

| Office | Term | Election Year Pattern |
|--------|------|----------------------|
| County Executive | 4 years | Odd-year elections |
| County Council (11 seats) | 4 years | Odd-year elections |

### City of Cleveland
Cleveland is a charter city with nonpartisan municipal elections.

| Office | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Mayor | Nonpartisan, 4-year term |
| City Council (17 wards) | Nonpartisan, 4-year term |
| Election type | Nonpartisan primary → nonpartisan general |
| Filing | City of Cleveland Charter governs |
| Campaign finance | State rules apply; no additional local limits |

### Cleveland-Specific Notes
- [ ] Municipal elections held in odd-numbered years
- [ ] Nonpartisan primary: top two advance to general
- [ ] No local contribution limits beyond state law
- [ ] File with Cuyahoga County Board of Elections

## Franklin County (Columbus)

### County Government
Franklin County operates under the statutory (non-charter) form with three
county commissioners elected at large.

| Office | Term | Election Pattern |
|--------|------|-----------------|
| County Commissioner | 4 years | Staggered, even-year partisan |
| Prosecutor, Auditor, etc. | 4 years | Even-year partisan |

### City of Columbus
Columbus is a charter city with a council-mayor structure.

| Office | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Mayor | Nonpartisan, 4-year term |
| City Council (7 at-large) | Nonpartisan, 4-year term |
| City Attorney | Nonpartisan, elected |
| City Auditor | Nonpartisan, elected |
| Election type | Nonpartisan primary → nonpartisan general |

### Columbus-Specific Notes
- [ ] Columbus voters approved a charter amendment creating a redistricting commission
- [ ] Municipal elections held in odd-numbered years
- [ ] Campaign finance governed by state law
- [ ] File with Franklin County Board of Elections

## Hamilton County (Cincinnati)

### County Government
Hamilton County uses the statutory three-commissioner model.

| Office | Term | Election Pattern |
|--------|------|-----------------|
| County Commissioner | 4 years | Staggered, even-year partisan |
| Row offices | 4 years | Even-year partisan |

### City of Cincinnati
Cincinnati uses a council-manager form of government with proportional
representation history (now repealed) and at-large council elections.

| Office | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Mayor | Selected by council from among members |
| City Council (9 at-large) | Nonpartisan, 4-year term |
| Election type | Top-9 from nonpartisan field |
| Filing | Hamilton County BOE |

### Cincinnati-Specific Notes
- [ ] Council members elected at-large (not by ward)
- [ ] Mayor chosen by council from its members
- [ ] Nonpartisan elections in odd-numbered years
- [ ] No additional local campaign finance limits

## School Board Elections

Ohio school board elections are **nonpartisan** and typically held in November of
odd-numbered years.

| Detail | Rule |
|--------|------|
| Partisan affiliation | Not listed on ballot |
| Term length | 4 years |
| Petition signatures | 25-50 valid signatures |
| Filing location | County board of elections |
| Campaign finance | Subject to state contribution limits |

### School Board Checklist
- [ ] File petition with county BOE
- [ ] Petitions available approximately 90 days before filing deadline
- [ ] No filing fee required
- [ ] Register campaign committee if raising/spending over threshold
- [ ] Nonpartisan ballot -- no party label

## Township Trustee Elections

Ohio has 1,308 townships governed by a three-member board of trustees.

| Detail | Rule |
|--------|------|
| Number of trustees | 3 per township |
| Term length | 4 years |
| Election type | Partisan (party label on ballot) |
| Petition requirement | 25 valid signatures |
| Filing location | County board of elections |
| Election timing | Odd-numbered years (November) |

### Township Fiscal Officer
Each township also elects a fiscal officer (4-year term, partisan).

## Municipal Charter Variations

Ohio charter cities can customize:
- Election dates (odd vs. even year)
- Partisan vs. nonpartisan elections
- Council structure (ward, at-large, or mixed)
- Recall provisions
- Initiative and referendum procedures

### Common Variations

| Feature | State Default | Charter Override Allowed? |
|---------|--------------|--------------------------|
| Partisan/nonpartisan | Partisan | Yes |
| Council structure | Varies | Yes |
| Term length | 4 years | Yes (2 or 4) |
| Recall | Not default | Yes |
| Initiative/referendum | Available | Can expand or modify |

## Local Campaign Finance Notes

- State contribution limits apply to all local races
- No Ohio municipality currently imposes contribution limits beyond state law
- Local candidates file campaign finance reports with the county BOE
- Same itemization thresholds apply ($100 individual, $25 political entities)
