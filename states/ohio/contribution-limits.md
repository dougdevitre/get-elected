# Ohio Contribution Limits (Detailed)

> **STALENESS WARNING:** This reference was written in April 2026. Ohio contribution
> limits are adjusted biennially based on the Consumer Price Index (CPI). The figures
> shown here reflect the 2025-2026 election cycle. Verify current limits at
> https://www.ohiosos.gov/campaign-finance/ before making compliance decisions.

> **EDUCATIONAL DISCLAIMER:** This document is for educational and informational purposes
> only. It does not constitute legal advice. Campaigns should consult a qualified election
> law attorney or the Ohio Secretary of State's office for guidance specific to their
> situation.

## Background

Ohio has maintained contribution limits since the 1970s, with periodic adjustments.
The limits are codified in Ohio Revised Code Chapter 3517 and are among the more
detailed frameworks in the Midwest:

- **Corporate contributions:** Ohio is one of the states that **bans** direct corporate
  treasury contributions to candidates. Corporations must form PACs to participate.
- **CPI adjustments:** Limits are adjusted every odd-numbered year based on CPI changes,
  rounded to the nearest dollar.
- **Per-election limits:** Each primary, general, and special election counts separately.

|--------|-----------|-------|
| Corporate treasury | **No** | Must form a PAC (separate segregated fund) |
| Union treasury | **No** | Must form a PAC |

Ohio is one of approximately 20 states that prohibit direct corporate and union
treasury contributions to candidates.

## Aggregate Limits

Ohio does **not** impose aggregate limits on the total amount a donor may give
across all candidates. A donor may give the maximum to every candidate in the
state without restriction.

---

## Self-Funding

Candidates may contribute **unlimited** amounts from their own personal funds to
their own campaign committee. Ohio has no "millionaire's amendment" or trigger
provision that adjusts limits when a candidate self-funds heavily.

---

## Special Rules and Exceptions

### In-Kind Contributions
In-kind contributions (goods, services, anything of value) count against the
contribution limit at fair market value.

### Loans
- Loans from financial institutions on commercially reasonable terms are not
  treated as contributions.
- Personal loans from individuals to candidates are contributions subject to limits.
- Candidate loans to their own campaign are unlimited.

### Cash Contributions
Cash contributions over $100 are prohibited. Contributions over $100 must be
made by check, credit card, or other traceable method.

### Anonymous Contributions
Contributions over $25 from anonymous sources are prohibited.

### Joint Fundraising
When multiple committees hold joint fundraisers, each donor's contribution is
allocated among the participating committees. Limits apply separately per recipient.

## Prohibited Contributions (Quick Reference)

| Source | Permitted? | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Individuals (including non-residents) | Yes | Subject to limits |
| Corporations | **No** | Must use PAC |
| Unions | **No** | Must use PAC |
| PACs | Yes | Subject to limits |
| Party committees | Yes | Higher limits apply |
| LLCs | **No** | Treated as corporate |
| Foreign nationals | **No** | Prohibited by state and federal law |
| Anonymous (over $25) | **No** | Must identify donor |
| Cash (over $100) | **No** | Must use traceable method |
| State contractors | Restricted | Limits on certain contracts |
