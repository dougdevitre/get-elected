# Texas Contribution Limits (Detailed)

> **STALENESS WARNING:** This reference was written in April 2026. Texas campaign finance
> rules may change through legislation. Judicial race limits under the Judicial Campaign
> Fairness Act are adjusted periodically. Verify current rules at
> https://www.ethics.state.tx.us before making compliance decisions.

> **EDUCATIONAL DISCLAIMER:** This document is for educational and informational purposes
> only. It does not constitute legal advice. Campaigns should consult a qualified election
> law attorney or the Texas Ethics Commission (TEC) for guidance specific to their situation.

---

## Background

Texas is one of a small number of states that does **not** impose contribution limits
on most races. There are no limits on individual, PAC, or party contributions to
candidates for legislative or executive offices. However, Texas does ban direct
corporate and union treasury contributions and imposes limits on judicial races through
the Judicial Campaign Fairness Act (JCFA).

Key history:
- **1903:** Texas enacted one of the nation's first corporate contribution bans.
- **1995:** The Judicial Campaign Fairness Act established contribution limits for
  judicial candidates.
- **Present:** No general contribution limits for non-judicial candidates.

---

## Non-Judicial Races: No Individual Contribution Limits

| Donor Type | To Statewide Candidate | To Legislative Candidate | To Local Candidate |
|------------|----------------------|------------------------|-------------------|
| Individual | **No limit** | **No limit** | **No limit** |
| PAC | **No limit** | **No limit** | **No limit** |
| Political party | **No limit** | **No limit** | **No limit** |
| Corporation (direct) | **Prohibited** | **Prohibited** | **Prohibited** |
| Union treasury (direct) | **Prohibited** | **Prohibited** | **Prohibited** |

Texas allows unlimited individual, PAC, and party committee contributions to candidates
for all non-judicial offices.

---

## Cash Contribution Limit

Regardless of the absence of general limits, Texas imposes a **$100 cash limit** on
contributions. Any contribution over $100 must be made by check, credit card, or other
traceable instrument.

---

## Corporate and Union Contribution Ban

- **Corporations:** May not contribute directly to candidates from corporate treasury
  funds. However, corporations may establish and fund a **PAC** (general-purpose
  committee), which may then contribute to candidates.
- **Unions:** May not contribute directly to candidates from union treasury funds.
  Unions may also establish PACs.
- **Professional associations and trade groups:** Same rules as corporations.
- **LLCs:** Treated as corporations for purposes of the contribution ban.
- **Sole proprietorships:** Contributions from a sole proprietor are treated as
  individual contributions (permitted, no limit).

---

## Judicial Campaign Fairness Act (JCFA) Limits

The JCFA imposes contribution limits on candidates for judicial office. These limits
vary by court level.

### Individual Contributions to Judicial Candidates

| Court | Limit Per Election |
|-------|--------------------|
| Statewide judicial office (Supreme Court, Court of Criminal Appeals) | $5,000 |
| Courts of Appeals | $5,000 |
| District Court | $5,000 |
| County Court-at-Law | $2,500 |
| Justice of the Peace | $2,500 |

### PAC Contributions to Judicial Candidates

| Court | Limit Per Election |
|-------|--------------------|
| Statewide judicial office | $5,000 |
| Courts of Appeals | $5,000 |
| District Court | $5,000 |
| County Court-at-Law | $2,500 |
| Justice of the Peace | $2,500 |

### Law Firm Aggregate Limits (JCFA)

The JCFA imposes aggregate limits on contributions from members of the same law firm
to a single judicial candidate:

| Court | Aggregate Limit Per Election (all members of a firm combined) |
|-------|--------------------------------------------------------------|
| Statewide judicial office | $50,000 |
| Courts of Appeals | $30,000 |
| District Court | $15,000 |
| County Court-at-Law | $15,000 |

### Voluntary Expenditure Limits (JCFA)

Judicial candidates may voluntarily agree to expenditure limits. Candidates who agree
receive a designation on the ballot indicating compliance. The limits vary by court
level and population of the jurisdiction.

---

## Self-Funding

- **Non-judicial candidates:** May contribute unlimited personal funds to their own
  campaigns.
- **Judicial candidates:** May contribute unlimited personal funds, but voluntary
  expenditure limits (if accepted) cap overall spending including self-funding.

---

## PAC Rules

### General-Purpose Committees (PACs)

- May accept unlimited contributions from individuals.
- May accept contributions from corporate and union treasuries (for PAC
  administrative/operating costs and for contributions to other PACs, but PAC
  contributions to candidates must come from permissible sources).
- May contribute unlimited amounts to non-judicial candidates.
- Subject to JCFA limits when contributing to judicial candidates.

### Specific-Purpose Committees

- Established to support or oppose a specific candidate, measure, or officeholder.
- Same contribution rules as general-purpose committees.
- Must register with the TEC.

---

## Political Party Contributions

| Donor | To Candidate |
|-------|-------------|
| State party committee | **No limit** (non-judicial) |
| County party committee | **No limit** (non-judicial) |
| To judicial candidates | Subject to JCFA limits |

---

## Prohibited Contributions (Quick Reference)

| Source | Permitted? | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Individuals | Yes | No limit (non-judicial); JCFA limits (judicial) |
| Corporations (direct) | **No** | Must use PAC |
| Unions (direct) | **No** | Must use PAC |
| PACs | Yes | No limit (non-judicial); JCFA limits (judicial) |
| Political parties | Yes | No limit (non-judicial) |
| LLCs | **No** | Treated as corporate |
| Foreign nationals | **No** | Prohibited |
| Anonymous (over $50) | **No** | Must identify donor |
| Cash (over $100) | **No** | Must use traceable instrument |

## Moratorium Periods

Texas law prohibits certain contributions during specific periods:

- **Statewide officeholders and candidates:** May not accept contributions during a
  legislative session (regular or special) -- this applies to the Governor, Lt. Governor,
  and other statewide officeholders.
- **Legislative members:** May not accept contributions during a regular legislative
  session or during the 30 days prior to a special session through adjournment.
- **Exceptions:** Contributions to retire debt from a previous election may be accepted
  during moratorium periods under certain conditions.
