# Texas Ballot Access Requirements

> **STALENESS WARNING:** This reference was written in April 2026. Filing deadlines,
> fees, and petition signature requirements may change through legislation. Always verify
> current requirements with the Texas Secretary of State at https://www.sos.texas.gov
> before filing.

> **EDUCATIONAL DISCLAIMER:** This document is for educational and informational purposes
> only. It does not constitute legal advice. Campaigns should consult a qualified election

## Overview

Texas uses a traditional partisan primary system. Major party candidates (Republican
and Democratic) file with their party and compete in a March primary. Independent and
third-party candidates follow separate petition-based processes for general election
ballot access.

|--------|-----------|
| Governor | $5,000 |
| Lieutenant Governor | $5,000 |
| Attorney General | $5,000 |
| Comptroller | $5,000 |
| Commissioner of General Land Office | $5,000 |
| Commissioner of Agriculture | $5,000 |
| Railroad Commissioner | $5,000 |
| U.S. Senator | $5,000 |
| U.S. Representative | $3,125 |

| District Judge | $1,500 - $2,500 |
| County Judge | $800 - $1,200 |
| Justice of the Peace | $375 - $750 |
| County Commissioner | $750 - $1,000 |
| Other county offices | $375 - $1,250 |

Filing fees vary for some offices based on the population of the jurisdiction. Check
with the county chair or Secretary of State for exact amounts.

### Petition Alternative

Candidates who cannot afford the filing fee may file a **petition in lieu of filing
fee**:

| Office | Signatures Required |
|--------|-------------------|
| Statewide offices | 5,000 |
| U.S. Representative | 500 |
| State Senator | 500 |
| State Representative | 500 |
| County/district offices | Varies (typically 50-250) |

Signatures must be from registered voters in the relevant jurisdiction who did not
vote in the other party's primary.

### Filing Deadline

- **Primary filing period:** Typically opens in mid-November and closes in mid-December
  of the year before the election.
- **Primary election:** First Tuesday in March (even years).
- **Primary runoff:** Typically late May.

## Independent Candidates

Independent candidates do not participate in party primaries and must petition for
general election ballot access.

| Office | Signatures Required | Deadline |
|--------|-------------------|----------|
| Statewide offices | 1% of votes cast in last Governor's race (~80,000+) | ~75 days after primary runoff |
| U.S. Representative | 500 | ~75 days after primary runoff |
| State Senator | 500 | ~75 days after primary runoff |
| State Representative | 500 | ~75 days after primary runoff |
| County/district offices | Varies | ~75 days after primary runoff |

### Petition Requirements

- Signers must be **registered voters** who did **not vote** in either party's primary
  or primary runoff that year.
- Petition circulators must be registered voters in Texas.
- Each petition page must include the circulator's affidavit.
- Petitions are filed with the Secretary of State (state/federal offices) or county
  judge (county offices).

## Third-Party Candidates

### New Party Qualification

A party that is not currently qualified may gain ballot access by:

- **Petition:** Gathering signatures equal to **1% of votes cast in the last
  Governor's race** from registered voters who did not participate in another party's
  primary. Filed by the January deadline in an even year.
- **Convention:** Hold a state convention and nominate candidates after the petition
  is verified.

### Established Minor Party Candidates

If a party is already qualified (received 2%+ of votes for Governor or another
statewide race), its candidates are nominated by convention and appear on the general
election ballot.

### Maintaining Party Status

A party maintains ballot access by receiving at least **2% of the vote** for any
statewide office in a general election.

---

## Write-In Candidates

- Write-in candidates must file a **Declaration of Write-In Candidacy**.
- **Deadline:** Typically 70 days before the general election (varies; check the
  Secretary of State's calendar).
- **Filing fee:** Same as the filing fee for the office (or petition alternative).
- Write-in votes are counted only for candidates who have filed a valid declaration.
- Write-in candidacy is available for the general election only (not primaries).

## Special Elections

Special elections to fill vacancies are called by the Governor (state offices) or
relevant authority (local offices). There is **no primary** for special elections --
all candidates appear on a single ballot. If no candidate receives a majority, a runoff
is held between the top two finishers.
