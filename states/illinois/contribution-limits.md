# Illinois Contribution Limits (Detailed)

> **STALENESS WARNING:** This reference was written in April 2026. Illinois contribution
> limits are set by statute and may change through legislation. The "limits-off" provision
> can dramatically alter the effective limits in specific races. Verify current limits at
> https://www.elections.il.gov before making compliance decisions.

> **EDUCATIONAL DISCLAIMER:** This document is for educational and informational purposes
> only. It does not constitute legal advice. Campaigns should consult a qualified election
> law attorney or the Illinois State Board of Elections (SBE) for guidance specific to
> their situation.

---

## Background

Illinois reinstated contribution limits in 2009 after operating without them for decades.
The current framework includes a unique "limits-off" provision that suspends all limits
in a race when a candidate self-funds above a threshold or when independent expenditures
exceed a threshold. Key features:

- **Limits enacted:** 2009 (Public Act 96-0832).
- **Limits-off provision:** Triggered by self-funding over $250,000 or independent
  expenditures over $100,000 in a race.
- **Corporate contributions:** Permitted (unlike many states).
- **No CPI adjustment:** Limits are statutory and do not automatically adjust for
  inflation.

---

## Current Limits

Limits apply **per election cycle** (the full period from the day after the last
general election through the next general election day).

### Individual Contributions to Candidates

| Office | Limit Per Election Cycle |
|--------|------------------------|
| Statewide offices (Governor, AG, SOS, Comptroller, Treasurer) | $5,800 |
| State Senate | $5,800 |
| State Representative | $5,800 |
| Local offices (county, municipal, etc.) | $5,800 |

### Corporate Contributions to Candidates

| Donor | Limit Per Election Cycle |
|-------|------------------------|
| Corporation to any candidate | $5,800 |

Illinois is one of the states that permits **direct corporate contributions** to
candidates, subject to the same limits as individuals.

### Union Contributions to Candidates

| Donor | Limit Per Election Cycle |
|-------|------------------------|
| Union to any candidate | $5,800 |

### PAC (Political Committee) Contributions to Candidates

| Donor | Limit Per Election Cycle |
|-------|------------------------|
| PAC to statewide candidate | $58,400 |
| PAC to legislative candidate | $58,400 |
| PAC to local candidate | $58,400 |

Political committees receive a **10x multiplier** on the individual limit.

### Political Party Contributions to Candidates

| Donor | Limit Per Election Cycle |
|-------|------------------------|
| State central committee to any candidate | **No limit** |
| Legislative caucus committee to any candidate | **No limit** |
| County/local party committee to any candidate | $58,400 |

State party and legislative caucus committees may contribute without limit.

### Contributions to Committees

| Donor Type | To a PAC | To a Political Party |
|------------|----------|---------------------|
| Individual | $5,800/year | $11,600/year |
| Corporation | $5,800/year | $11,600/year |
| Union | $5,800/year | $11,600/year |
| PAC to PAC | $58,400/year | $58,400/year |
| Candidate committee to PAC | $58,400/year | $58,400/year |

## Limits-Off Provision

Illinois has a unique provision that **removes all contribution limits** in a specific
race when certain thresholds are triggered:

### Triggers

| Trigger | Threshold |
|---------|----------|
| Self-funding by any candidate in the race | $250,000 or more of personal funds |
| Independent expenditures in the race | $100,000 or more (for or against any candidate) |

### How It Works

1. When a trigger occurs, the SBE is notified.
2. The SBE declares "limits off" for that specific race.
3. **All candidates** in the affected race may then accept unlimited contributions
   from any permissible source.
4. Limits remain off for the remainder of that election cycle for that race.
5. This applies to both the primary and general election for the affected race.

### Practical Impact

- The limits-off provision means that in competitive or high-profile races (especially
  gubernatorial races), contribution limits are frequently suspended.
- Candidates and their opponents should monitor SBE notices for limits-off declarations.

---

## Self-Funding

- Candidates may contribute unlimited personal funds to their own campaign.
- **However:** Self-funding of $250,000 or more triggers the limits-off provision for
  all candidates in that race.
- Self-funded contributions must be reported.

---

## Transfer Rules

- **Between candidate committees:** A candidate committee may transfer funds to another
  candidate committee, subject to the PAC-to-candidate limit ($58,400).
- **Between party committees:** Transfers between party committees at different levels
  are subject to specific transfer limits.
- **Candidate to party:** A candidate committee may transfer to a party committee
  subject to the candidate-to-party limit.

## Prohibited Contributions (Quick Reference)

| Source | Permitted? | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Individuals | Yes | $5,800/cycle to candidates |
| Corporations | Yes | $5,800/cycle to candidates |
| Unions | Yes | $5,800/cycle to candidates |
| PACs | Yes | $58,400/cycle to candidates |
| State party / caucus committees | Yes | **No limit** to candidates |
| County party committees | Yes | $58,400/cycle to candidates |
| LLCs | Yes | Treated as corporate (subject to limits) |
| Foreign nationals | **No** | Prohibited |
| Anonymous (over $150) | **No** | Must identify donor |
| Cash (over $150) | **No** | Must use traceable instrument |
| State contractors | Restricted | Limits on contributions from holders of state contracts |

---

## Aggregate Limits

Illinois does **not** impose aggregate limits on the total amount an individual may give
across all candidates and committees.

---

## In-Kind Contributions

In-kind contributions count against the applicable limit at fair market value. This
includes donated goods, services, and use of property or facilities.

---

## Loans

- Loans from a candidate to their own campaign are unlimited (but may trigger
  limits-off).
- Loans from other individuals are treated as contributions subject to limits.
- Bank loans on commercially reasonable terms are not treated as contributions.
- Loans must be reported and are considered outstanding debts until repaid.
