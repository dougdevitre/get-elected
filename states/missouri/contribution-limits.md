# Missouri Contribution Limits (Detailed)

> **STALENESS WARNING:** This reference was written in April 2026. Missouri contribution
> limits are adjusted biennially based on the Consumer Price Index (CPI). The figures
> shown here reflect the 2025-2026 election cycle. Verify current limits at
> https://www.mec.mo.gov before making compliance decisions.

> **EDUCATIONAL DISCLAIMER:** This document is for educational and informational purposes
> only. It does not constitute legal advice. Campaigns should consult a qualified election
> law attorney or the Missouri Ethics Commission for guidance specific to their situation.

---

## Background

Missouri's contribution limits have a turbulent history:

- **Pre-2008:** Missouri had contribution limits in place.
- **2008:** The Missouri Supreme Court struck down contribution limits. For the next
  eight years, Missouri was a "no limits" state -- one of only a handful nationwide.
- **August 2016:** Missouri voters approved Constitutional Amendment 2, reinstating
  contribution limits effective immediately.
- **2017-present:** Limits have been adjusted biennially for CPI per the amendment's
  provisions.

## Current Limits (2025-2026 Cycle)

All limits are **per election**. The primary, general, and any runoff each count as
separate elections. A donor may give up to the limit for each election in which the
candidate participates.

### Individual Contributions to Candidates

| Office | Limit Per Election |
|--------|--------------------|
| Governor | $2,875 |
| Lieutenant Governor | $2,875 |
| Secretary of State | $2,875 |
| State Treasurer | $2,875 |
| Attorney General | $2,875 |
| State Auditor | $2,875 |
| State Senator | $2,875 |
| State Representative | $2,875 |
| All other offices (county, municipal, judicial) | $2,875 |

### Committee (PAC) Contributions to Candidates

| Office | Limit Per Election |
|--------|--------------------|
| Statewide offices | $2,875 |
| State Senate | $2,875 |
| State Representative | $2,875 |
| All other offices | $2,875 |

### Corporate and Union Contributions to Candidates

Missouri is one of the states that allows direct corporate and union treasury
contributions to candidates. The same per-election limits apply:

| Office | Limit Per Election |
|--------|--------------------|
| All offices | $2,875 |

### Party Committee Contributions to Candidates

| Donor | Limit |
|-------|-------|
| State party committee | **Unlimited** |
| County/local party committee | **Unlimited** |
| National party committee | **Unlimited** |

Party committees at all levels are exempt from the per-election contribution limits
when giving to candidates. This is a significant channel for campaign funding in
Missouri.

### Contributions to Committees (PACs)

| Donor Type | To a PAC |
|------------|----------|
| Individual | **No limit** |
| Corporation | **No limit** |
| Union | **No limit** |
| PAC to PAC | **No limit** |

There is no limit on contributions to committees (PACs). The limits apply only to
contributions **from** any source **to** candidate committees.

### Contributions to Party Committees

| Donor Type | To a Party Committee |
|------------|---------------------|
| Individual | **No limit** |
| Corporation | **No limit** |
| Union | **No limit** |
| PAC | **No limit** |

## CPI Adjustment Mechanism

- Constitutional Amendment 2 set base limits at $2,600 (effective August 2016).
- Limits are recalculated every two years based on cumulative CPI changes since 2016.
- Adjusted amounts are rounded to the nearest $25.
- The MEC publishes updated limits before each election cycle.

### Historical Limits

| Cycle | Per-Election Limit (All Offices) |
|-------|----------------------------------|
| 2016 (base) | $2,600 |
| 2017-2018 | $2,600 |
| 2019-2020 | $2,700 |
| 2021-2022 | $2,750 |
| 2023-2024 | $2,850 |
| 2025-2026 | $2,875 |

## Special Rules and Exceptions

### Self-Funding
Candidates may contribute unlimited amounts from their own personal funds to their
own campaign committee. There is no self-funding trigger that modifies limits for
other donors (unlike some states with "millionaire's amendment" provisions).

### Aggregate Limits
Missouri does **not** impose aggregate limits. A donor may give the maximum to every
candidate in the state without hitting a cap.

### In-Kind Contributions
In-kind contributions (goods, services, anything of value other than money) count
against the contribution limit at fair market value.

### Loans
- Loans from financial institutions made in the ordinary course of business are not
  treated as contributions (provided they are on commercially reasonable terms).
- Loans from individuals are treated as contributions subject to limits.
- Candidate loans to their own campaign are unlimited (treated as self-funding).

### Joint Fundraising
When multiple committees hold joint fundraising events, each contributor's donation
is allocated among the participating committees. The contribution limit applies
separately to each recipient committee.

### Returning/Refunding Contributions
If a campaign receives an excessive contribution, it must return the excess within
a reasonable time. The returned amount should be reported on the next campaign
finance report.

## Prohibited Contributions (Quick Reference)

| Source | Permitted? | Notes |
|--------|-----------|-------|
| Individuals (including non-residents) | Yes | Subject to limits |
| Corporations | Yes | Subject to limits |
| Unions | Yes | Subject to limits |
| PACs | Yes | Subject to limits |
| Party committees | Yes | **No limit** |
| LLCs | Yes | Treated as the entity type (corporate or individual) |
| Foreign nationals | **No** | Prohibited |
| Anonymous (over $100) | **No** | Under $100 permitted but tracked |
| Cash (over $100) | **No** | Must use traceable payment method |
| Lobbyists (during session) | **No** | To legislative candidates during session |
| Gaming licensees | **No** | To certain state officials |
