## Overview

Missouri ballot access rules differ based on whether a candidate is seeking nomination
through a major party primary, running as an independent, or running as a third-party
candidate. Major party candidates (Democratic and Republican) have a straightforward
filing process with a fee but no petition requirement. Independent and third-party
candidates must collect petition signatures.

### Filing Process for Major Party Candidates

1. **Obtain a Declaration of Candidacy form** from the Secretary of State (for state
   and federal offices) or the county clerk (for county offices).
2. **Complete the Declaration of Candidacy**, including the office sought, party
   affiliation, and personal information.
3. **Pay the filing fee** at the time of filing.
4. **File in person or by mail** at the Secretary of State's office (state/federal
   offices) or county clerk's office (county offices) before the deadline.
5. **File a Statement of Committee Organization** (MCE-1) with the MEC if not already
   registered.

## Independent Candidates

Independent candidates do not participate in party primaries. They must collect petition
signatures to appear on the general election ballot.

| Office | Signatures Required | Filing Deadline |
|--------|-------------------|-----------------|
| Statewide offices | 10,000 registered voters | Last Monday in July |
| U.S. Representative | 2% of votes cast in last election for that office | Last Monday in July |
| State Senator | 2% of votes cast in last election for that office | Last Monday in July |
| State Representative | 2% of votes cast in last election for that office | Last Monday in July |
| County offices | Varies by county (typically 2%) | Last Monday in July |

### Petition Requirements

- Signatures must be from **registered voters** in the relevant district/jurisdiction.
- Petition circulators must be registered voters in Missouri.
- Each petition page must include the circulator's affidavit.
- Signatures are verified by the relevant election authority.
- Petitions must be filed with the Secretary of State (state/federal offices) or
  county clerk (county offices).

## Third-Party Candidates

Third parties (parties other than Democratic and Republican) must first establish ballot
access for the party, then nominate candidates.

### New Party Qualification

A political party qualifies for ballot access by:
- Gathering petition signatures equal to **2% of the total votes cast** in the last
  gubernatorial election (statewide), OR
- Having a candidate receive at least **2% of the vote** in the last gubernatorial
  election.

Once qualified, the party may nominate candidates by convention or committee (not by
primary, unless the party opts in to the primary system).

### Maintaining Party Status

A party maintains its ballot access if at least one of its candidates receives **2%
of the vote** in a statewide general election.

---

## Write-In Candidates

- Write-in candidates **must file a declaration of intent** with the appropriate
  election authority **before the election**.
- The deadline for filing a write-in declaration is typically the **second Friday
  before the election**.
- Write-in votes are only counted for candidates who have filed a valid declaration.
- Write-in candidates are not required to pay a filing fee.

## Special Elections

Special elections to fill vacancies follow different timelines set by the Governor's
proclamation or relevant authority. Filing deadlines and petition requirements for
special elections are specified in the proclamation. Candidates should contact the
Secretary of State or county clerk for special election filing procedures.
