# California Ballot Access Requirements

> **STALENESS WARNING:** This reference was written in April 2026. Filing deadlines,
> fees, and petition signature requirements may change through legislation. Always verify
> current requirements with the California Secretary of State at https://www.sos.ca.gov
> before filing.

> **EDUCATIONAL DISCLAIMER:** This document is for educational and informational purposes
> only. It does not constitute legal advice. Campaigns should consult a qualified election
> law attorney or the Secretary of State's office for guidance specific to their situation.

---

## Overview

California uses a **top-two primary** system (also called "jungle primary") for most
state and federal offices. All candidates, regardless of party, appear on a single
primary ballot. The top two vote-getters advance to the general election, even if they
are from the same party. Presidential races and local nonpartisan offices have different
rules.

---

## Top-Two Primary System

- **Applies to:** Governor, Lieutenant Governor, Attorney General, Secretary of State,
  Controller, Treasurer, Insurance Commissioner, Superintendent of Public Instruction,
  Board of Equalization, State Senate, State Assembly, U.S. Senate, U.S. Representative.
- **Does NOT apply to:** President, local nonpartisan offices, judicial offices.
- **Key feature:** Party endorsement appears on ballot, but all voters may vote for any
  candidate regardless of party registration.
- **Advancement:** Only the top two vote-getters advance to the general election.
- **Write-ins:** Write-in candidates are permitted in the primary but **not** in the
  general election (under the top-two system).

## Filing Methods

Candidates may access the ballot through **filing fees** or **petition signatures**
(or a combination).

### Option 1: Filing Fee

| Office | Filing Fee |
|--------|-----------|
| Governor | 2% of annual salary (~$4,500) |
| Other statewide constitutional offices | 2% of annual salary |
| State Senator | 1% of annual salary (~$1,200) |
| State Assembly Member | 1% of annual salary (~$1,200) |
| U.S. Senator | 2% of annual salary |
| U.S. Representative | 1% of annual salary |
| Board of Equalization | 1% of annual salary |

Filing fees are nonrefundable. Exact amounts are set each cycle based on the current
salary for the office.

### Option 2: Petition in Lieu of Filing Fee

Candidates may collect petition signatures instead of (or to offset) the filing fee.

| Office | Signatures Required (in lieu of full fee) |
|--------|------------------------------------------|
| Governor | 7,000 - 10,000 |
| Other statewide offices | 5,000 - 7,000 |
| State Senator | 1,500 - 3,000 |
| State Assembly Member | 1,000 - 1,500 |
| U.S. Senator | 7,000 - 10,000 |
| U.S. Representative | 1,000 - 3,000 |

Signatures must be from registered voters in the relevant jurisdiction. Partial
petition submissions reduce the filing fee proportionally.

### Option 3: Combination

A candidate may submit a partial petition and pay a reduced filing fee for the
remaining balance.

## Filing Deadlines

| Event | Typical Timing |
|-------|---------------|
| Filing period opens | ~113 days before primary |
| Filing period closes (with incumbent) | ~88 days before primary |
| Extended filing period (no incumbent) | ~83 days before primary |
| Petition in lieu filing period | ~148 to ~113 days before primary |
| Primary election | First Tuesday after first Monday in June (even years) |
| General election | First Tuesday after first Monday in November |

Exact dates are published by the Secretary of State for each election cycle.

## Candidate Statement of Qualifications

Candidates may submit a **Statement of Qualifications** (candidate statement) to be
included in the official voter information guide mailed to all voters.

- **Length:** Up to 250 words.
- **Cost:** Candidates must pay the printing and mailing cost, which varies by district
  size (can range from a few hundred dollars to over $20,000 for statewide offices).
- **Exception:** Candidates who accept the voluntary expenditure ceiling (AB 571) for
  legislative races have their candidate statement included at no cost.
- **Deadline:** Filed with the Declaration of Candidacy during the filing period.

---

## Write-In Candidates

- **Primary election:** Write-in candidates are permitted. Must file a Statement of
  Write-In Candidacy and pay a filing fee (same as regular filing fee).
- **General election:** Write-in candidates are **not permitted** under the top-two
  primary system for offices covered by that system.
- **Exceptions:** Write-in candidates are permitted in presidential general elections
  and some local races.

## Residency and Eligibility Requirements

| Office | Minimum Age | Residency Requirement |
|--------|------------|----------------------|
| Governor | 18 | U.S. citizen, 5 years California resident |
| Lieutenant Governor | 18 | U.S. citizen, 5 years California resident |
| Attorney General | 18 | Admitted to California Bar for 5 years |
| Secretary of State | 18 | U.S. citizen, California resident |
| Controller | 18 | U.S. citizen, California resident |
| Treasurer | 18 | U.S. citizen, California resident |
| State Senator | 18 | U.S. citizen, California resident, 1 year district resident |
| State Assembly Member | 18 | U.S. citizen, California resident, 1 year district resident |
| U.S. Senator | 30 | California resident, 9 years U.S. citizen |
| U.S. Representative | 25 | California resident, 7 years U.S. citizen |

## Term Limits

| Office | Term Limit |
|--------|-----------|
| Governor | 2 terms (8 years) |
| Other statewide offices | 2 terms per office |
| State Senator | 12 years total in legislature (combined Senate + Assembly) |
| State Assembly Member | 12 years total in legislature (combined) |

The 12-year combined limit was established by Proposition 28 (2012), replacing the
previous separate limits of 2 Senate terms and 3 Assembly terms.

---

## Special Elections

Special elections to fill vacancies follow timelines set by the Governor's proclamation.
The top-two primary system applies to special elections for state and federal offices.
Filing deadlines and procedures are specified in the proclamation.

---

## Local Nonpartisan Offices

Local offices (city council, school board, special district) are typically nonpartisan
and do not use the top-two primary system. Rules vary by jurisdiction:

- **Filing fees and petitions:** Set by local ordinance or charter.
- **Election timing:** Many local elections are consolidated with statewide elections;
  some jurisdictions hold elections in odd-numbered years.
- **Runoffs:** Some cities use runoff elections if no candidate receives a majority.
